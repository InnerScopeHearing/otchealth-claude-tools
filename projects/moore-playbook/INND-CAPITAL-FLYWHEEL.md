## 1. The flywheel, in one paragraph
INND is the public-company roof — the "Launch Platform." Real operating traction at OTCHealth
(reignition, TReO, the OTC line, subscriptions, the app portfolio) becomes a **legitimate,
Reg-FD-compliant public story**. That story, properly disclosed, supports **public capital**
(a sequence of exemptions) and makes the stock usable as **acquisition currency** for a
**roll-up** of complementary hearing/senior-health businesses. Capital + currency fund more
operating capacity → more traction → repeat. **The two engines stay strictly separate in
execution; the wall is what keeps the flywheel legitimate.**

## 2. The capital sequence (the vehicle ladder)
Each rung serves a different investor pool and timeline. The fleet's `raise-ops` vehicle-matrix
already frames this; below is the regulatory reality each rung must satisfy, with our intended
use. **Counsel selects, structures, and runs every one.**

### Rung 1 — Reg D 506(c) · accredited, fastest cash (the live lever)
- Accredited investors **only**; **general solicitation permitted**; **no dollar cap**.
- Issuer must take **"reasonable steps to verify"** accredited status (self-certification NOT
  allowed under 506(c)). A March-2025 SEC no-action letter allows a high-minimum-investment
  path ($200K/natural person, $1M/entity + a written no-third-party-financing rep) as one
  verification method; document review (tax returns, CPA/attorney/BD letters) remains valid.
- **Form D** within 15 days of first sale. Securities are **restricted** (Rule 144: 6-month
  hold for a current reporting company; affiliates face volume/manner limits).
- **Our use:** the live Series C tranche — accredited contacts, data room ready. Fastest path
  to cash, days-to-weeks once counsel clears.
- *Alternative Rung 1b — 506(b):* no general solicitation, but allows up to 35 sophisticated
  non-accredited investors with a pre-existing relationship; reasonable-belief verification.

### Rung 2 — Reg CF / WeFunder · the retail crowd flywheel
- **Up to $5,000,000 per rolling 12 months.** Open to **retail** investors (non-accredited
  investment limits apply; accredited unlimited).
- Must run through **one SEC-registered funding portal / broker-dealer** (e.g. WeFunder) — the
  issuer cannot run it directly. **Form C** on EDGAR before launch; **Form C-U** progress
  updates; **Form C-AR** annual report.
- **Financial-statement tiers:** ≤$124K self-certified; $124K–$618K reviewed CPA; $618K–$1.235M
  reviewed (first-time) / audited (repeat); >$1.235M audited.
- **"Testing the Waters"** (gauge interest, accept no money) is permitted pre-launch — the
  documented reservation-funnel play (build 1,500–3,000 interested leads off the owned list
  before launch). All TTW materials file with the Form C.
- **Our use:** activate the shareholder/customer base as retail believers; the broad-funnel
  complement to 506(c). 8–12 week prep.

### Rung 3 — Reg A+ · the larger, later raise
- **Tier 1: up to $20M / 12 mo** (no SEC-ongoing reporting, but **no blue-sky preemption** —
  must clear every state; heavy). **Tier 2: up to $75M / 12 mo**, **audited financials**,
  **blue-sky preempted** for qualified purchasers, ongoing reporting (**1-K** annual, **1-SA**
  semiannual, **1-U** current).
- **Form 1-A** must be **SEC-qualified before sales** (≈3–5 months for an initial filing); TTW
  permitted. Tier 2 non-accredited natural persons limited to 10% of greater of income/net
  worth per offering.
- **Our use:** the scale instrument once traction + audited financials support it — later phase.

verification method, Rule 506(d) bad-actor checks, and all filings. The fleet only drafts

## 3. The reverse split (FINRA Rule 6490)
- **What it does:** proportionally reduces shares outstanding and raises per-share price.
  **What it does NOT do:** change total market cap, equity, or economic value — a holder's
  total position value is mathematically unchanged. It is a common **uplisting prerequisite**
  (e.g. Nasdaq minimum-bid thresholds) and does not cure underlying business issues; prices can
  drift back.
- **Process (OTC issuer):** board authorizes + amends the charter → submit FINRA Company-Related
  Action Notification + signed board resolution + charter amendment + **Transfer-Agent
  Verification Form** (TA submits simultaneously) → fee (timely 10b-17 notice = $200; late
  escalates to $1,000/$2,000/$5,000) → FINRA reviews, may request more docs, may decline if
  deficiency factors exist (e.g. not current in SEC reporting) → on processing, published on the
  Daily List + price adjusted. **Required ≥10 calendar days before the record date.**
- **Status / context:** PRIORITIES frames a reverse split as part of the Launch-Platform
  repositioning, "IN MOTION (longer)," FINRA-6490 path estimated 3–12 months once formally
  initiated. **Public historical fact:** INND executed a 1-for-2500 reverse split on
  **2024-08-22** (public record). Any *future* reverse split is a separate board/counsel action;
  whether/when/ratio are **not decided here**.

**COUNSEL GATE (§3):** board + counsel decide whether, when, and at what ratio; counsel + the
transfer agent run the 6490 process. COO does not initiate.

## 4. The roll-up thesis (the Launch Platform compounding lever)
**The idea:** use INND as the public vehicle to acquire complementary hearing / senior-health /
adjacent brands, paying in **stock** rather than cash, and compound enterprise value.

- **Stock as currency — two paths:** (a) **registered** stock-for-stock merger via **Form S-4**
  (combines registration + proxy/prospectus; needs audited target financials + pro formas;
  ≈3–5 months to effectiveness); or (b) **exempt** share issuance to a small set of accredited
  target shareholders under **Reg D 506** (restricted shares, Rule 144 hold, usually with
  contractual registration rights). Counsel chooses per deal.
- **Multiple-arbitrage logic (why roll-ups create value):** a public acquirer trading at a
  higher revenue/EBITDA multiple acquires private targets at a **lower** multiple; once the
  target's earnings consolidate and re-rate at the acquirer's multiple, value is created from
  the **spread alone**, before any synergies. Pairs directly with the Moore Playbook's
  "subscription-mix re-rating" lever — recurring revenue earns a higher multiple than hardware.
- **Accretion basics:** a stock deal is EPS-accretive when the target's per-new-share earnings
  contribution exceeds the acquirer's EPS — i.e. **cheap targets + a higher-multiple acquirer**.
  Roll-ups work best when the acquirer's own stock carries a healthy multiple (a reason
  uplisting + the subscription re-rate matter before an aggressive roll-up).
- **Risks to design around (all counsel/CFO territory):** SEC disclosure on material
  acquisitions (Rule 3-05 audited target financials on an 8-K within 75 days above significance
  thresholds); integration underperformance; **goodwill impairment** (ASC 350) if targets miss;
  a **dilution spiral** if stock-funded deals don't earn out and the price falls; and
  **OTC-specific** limits — thin liquidity makes stock a weaker currency and draws heightened
  SEC scrutiny, so **uplisting materially improves the roll-up dynamic.**

**COUNSEL GATE (§4):** no target outreach, LOI, valuation, or share issuance without Matt +
counsel + CFO. The fleet may only build a non-binding target-landscape/screen.

## 5. The securities firewall + IR rules (always on)
The fleet's `ir-support` skill is **draft-only**; every output carries an "ATTORNEY REVIEW
REQUIRED" flag and the fixed gate chain: **agent drafts → counsel reviews → Matt approves →
then (and only then) sent/filed.** Standing rules the fleet enforces:
- **Reg FD:** no selective disclosure of MNPI — material news goes out broadly, never to a
  subset.
- **No-promotion:** no share-price talk, no "undervalued/buy/about to move," nothing aimed at
  driving liquidity. Factual disclosure only.
- **PSLRA safe harbor:** forward-looking statements carry the required cautionary language.
- **Section 17(b):** any compensated IR/promotion disclosed.
- **Rule 144 / affiliates:** the Moores are affiliates; all trading/resale is **counsel-only**;
  the fleet never advises or facilitates trading.
- **FINRA 24-09 / quiet-period discipline:** product-growth activity stays strictly walled from
  anything touching the public company; product marketing never references share price or
  capital structure.

**COUNSEL GATE (§5):** counsel owns all disclosure judgments; the COO never publishes
investor/IR/INND content.
