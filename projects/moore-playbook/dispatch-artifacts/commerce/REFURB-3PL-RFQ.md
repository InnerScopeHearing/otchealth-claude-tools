## A. Purpose and what we are buying

We hold approximately 10,298 owned units of TReO and legacy personal sound amplifier inventory and accessories. We are selecting one refurbishment and third party logistics (3PL) partner to:

1. Receive, inspect, and grade the pool.
2. Cosmetically and functionally recondition sellable units to a defined standard.
3. Store, pick, pack, and ship refurbished units to our sales channels and direct to consumer.
4. Receive and process customer returns from our retail channels (this is also where TReO sales returns physically route).

We are issuing this RFQ to three qualified vendors and will award based on the responses plus a paid 100-unit pilot. We are funding the pilot and the program from first product revenue, so vendor pricing and throughput clarity matter directly to our go decision.

## B. Scope of work (what we are asking each vendor to quote)

### B1. Inbound receiving and inspection
- Receive palletized inbound inventory at the vendor facility.
- Per-unit intake scan and inventory reconciliation against our manifest.
- Inbound inspection of every unit against the grading criteria in Section C.
- Report intake grade distribution back to us within a defined SLA.

### B2. Refurbishment and quality control
- Clean, recondition, and function-test each sellable unit to the QC pass criteria in Section C.
- Replace consumable and wear parts as needed (domes, tubes, ear tips, batteries) from parts we supply or the vendor sources at a quoted cost.
- Repackage to a defined retail-ready or value-pack standard.
- Quarantine and document units that fail QC, with a disposition recommendation (parts, scrap, or return to us).

### B3. Warehousing and fulfillment (3PL)
- Store finished refurbished units and raw pool inventory.
- Pick, pack, and ship to our channels (direct to consumer, marketplace fulfillment prep, and bulk to retail) on receipt of orders via our systems.
- Provide tracking and inventory visibility.

### B4. Returns handling
- Receive customer returns from our channels.
- Triage returns: restock to refurbished stock if passing QC, route to re-refurbishment if recoverable, or quarantine and report.
- Provide a returns disposition report on a defined cadence.
- This is the physical destination for TReO retail returns, so returns handling is a required, not optional, line.

## C. Inbound inspection and QC criteria (the grading standard)

Vendors must price against this standard. We will finalize exact thresholds jointly during the pilot, but the framework is fixed.

**Intake grading (assign every unit one grade):**
- **Grade A** — fully functional, cosmetically clean or near-new, retail sellable after light reconditioning.
- **Grade B** — fully functional, cosmetic wear, sellable as refurbished after standard reconditioning and repackaging.
- **Grade C** — functional fault recoverable with a consumable or wear-part replacement, sellable after repair.
- **Grade D** — not economically recoverable, route to parts or scrap with documentation.

**QC pass criteria (a unit may not ship as refurbished unless all pass):**
- Powers on and holds charge or runs on a fresh battery to spec.
- Amplification and sound output function across the expected range, no dead channel, no audible distortion at test settings.
- Controls and switches operate.
- Housing intact, no cracks, cleaned and sanitized.
- All wear parts (domes, tubes, tips) new or verified good.
- Repackaged complete with required accessories per our pack spec.

**Documentation:** per-unit pass or fail logged, grade recorded, and a quarantine log for all fails with reason codes.

## D. The questions every vendor must answer (the quote sheet)

Please respond to each item. Incomplete responses cannot be scored.

1. **Per-unit refurbishment cost.** Quote the fully loaded cost per unit to take a Grade B unit to QC-pass refurbished and repackaged. Provide separate adders for Grade C repair and for parts you source.
2. **Inbound inspection cost.** Cost per unit to receive, scan, grade, and reconcile against our manifest.
3. **Throughput.** Units inspected per day and units refurbished to QC-pass per day at steady state. State ramp time to reach that rate from a standing start.
4. **Storage cost.** Per-pallet or per-unit-per-month storage for both raw pool and finished refurbished stock.
5. **Fulfillment cost.** Pick, pack, and ship cost per order for a single-unit direct-to-consumer shipment and for a bulk-to-retail shipment.
6. **Returns handling cost.** Per-return cost to receive, triage, and either restock, re-refurbish, or quarantine, plus the returns disposition reporting cadence you provide.
7. **QC pass rate experience.** On comparable consumer electronics or small-device reconditioning, what intake-to-sellable yield do you typically see by grade.
8. **Lead time SLAs.** Inbound receiving SLA, refurb turnaround SLA, ship-by SLA, and returns processing SLA.
9. **Systems and visibility.** What inventory and order systems you run, what integration you support (API, EDI, marketplace, Shopify), and what real-time visibility we get.
10. **Minimums and term.** Minimum monthly volume, onboarding fee, contract term, and termination terms.
11. **Capacity for the full pool.** Confirm you can absorb the full approximately 10,298-unit pool and state the timeline to process it at your quoted throughput.
12. **References.** Two references for comparable refurbishment and 3PL work.

**Format:** please return a single quote sheet with line-item pricing plus a one-page narrative on throughput and onboarding. We will shortlist and award a paid 100-unit pilot before any full-pool commitment.

## F. 100-Unit Pilot SOW outline (for Matt to authorize)

**Objective:** prove the partner on a small, paid, low-risk batch before committing the pool. Produce real per-unit cost, real yield, and real throughput numbers to plug into the CFO unit-economics model and to set the refurbished sell price.

**Scope of the pilot:**
- We ship 100 mixed units from the pool to the selected vendor.
- Vendor receives, scans, and grades all 100 against Section C and reports the grade distribution.
- Vendor refurbishes the Grade A, B, and C units to QC-pass and repackages them.
- Vendor returns a per-unit log: intake grade, QC result, parts used, and final disposition.
- Vendor ships a sample of the finished units back to us for our own QC verification.
- Vendor processes 5 simulated returns end to end so we see the returns loop work.

**Pilot deliverables from the vendor:**
- Actual per-unit refurbishment cost realized on the 100 units.
- Actual intake-to-sellable yield by grade.
- Actual throughput (units per day) observed.
- The per-unit log and the returns disposition report.

**Our acceptance criteria:**
- Yield and per-unit cost are within the range that supports a refurbished sell price in the $199 to $299 band at an acceptable contribution margin (CFO confirms against the unit-econ model).
- Finished units pass our own QC verification.
- The returns loop completed cleanly.
- Systems visibility was real and usable.

**Pilot commercials (Matt authorizes):**
- Fixed pilot fee = vendor per-unit refurb quote times 100, plus inbound inspection on 100 units, plus return shipping of the sample, agreed in writing before units ship.
- Funded from first product revenue. Do not authorize the pilot spend before first dollars unless Matt explicitly chooses to pre-fund.

**Pilot gates and ownership:**
- Commerce lead: runs the RFQ, selects the vendor, manages the pilot, reports results.
- Matt: authorizes the pilot fee and the full-pool award. Physical-gate items (signing the vendor contract, releasing payment) are Matt actions.
- CFO: confirms the pilot numbers against the unit-econ model and sets the refurbished sell price.
- COO/CS: confirms the returns loop meets the brand-health refund and reachability SOP, since TReO retail returns route to the same partner.

**Exit of the pilot:** a go or no-go on the full approximately 10,298-unit program with real per-unit cost, yield, throughput, and a confirmed refurbished sell price, ready to scale inventory monetization.
