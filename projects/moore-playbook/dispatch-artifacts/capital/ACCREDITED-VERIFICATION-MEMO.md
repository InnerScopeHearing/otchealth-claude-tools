# Accredited-Investor Verification Framework + Rule 506(d) Bad-Actor Questionnaire Outline

> **ATTORNEY REVIEW REQUIRED.** This is an internal **framework memo** describing, at a general level, the categories of reasonable steps a Rule 506(c) issuer may take to verify accredited-investor status, plus an **outline** of a Rule 506(d) bad-actor questionnaire. It is **not** legal advice, **not** a verification procedure that may be used as-is, and **not** an offer or solicitation. The actual verification method, the questionnaire wording, the evidence standards, and the recordkeeping are all selected and approved by retained securities counsel.

> **COUNSEL GATE.** No verification of any investor occurs, and no questionnaire is sent, until (1) securities counsel is retained and approves the method and forms, and (2) the key-rotation hard gate is GREEN (see footer). The Capital seat prepares and flags; it does not verify, does not collect investor evidence, and does not solicit.

> **SCOPE.** Framework-level only. No investor names, no figures, no thresholds stated as operative dollar amounts, no share data. The plain-English category descriptions below are for orientation; **the operative legal standards, dollar thresholds, and acceptable evidence are exactly as counsel specifies under the current SEC rules.** Non-PHI ring; investor diligence is never co-mingled with any PHI system.

---

## Part A — Why 506(c) requires verification (orientation)

A Rule 506(c) offering permits **general solicitation**, but in exchange the issuer must take **reasonable steps to verify** that every purchaser is an accredited investor. A self-certification check-the-box (which is acceptable in a 506(b) offering) is **not sufficient** for 506(c). This is the single defining compliance obligation of the 506(c) lane, and getting it wrong can cost the exemption. **Counsel selects the method and sets the evidentiary bar.**

## Part B — Categories of reasonable steps (framework only; counsel selects)

The SEC describes both a **principles-based** approach (an objective determination based on the particular facts and circumstances of each purchaser and transaction) and a set of **non-exclusive safe-harbor methods.** The categories below are descriptive; counsel decides which to use and how.

### B.1 Principles-based (facts-and-circumstances) approach
- An objective assessment of whether the steps taken were reasonable given the type of purchaser, the information the issuer has about them, and the nature/terms of the offering.
- Counsel defines what mix of evidence is reasonable for each purchaser category and documents the rationale.

### B.2 Non-exclusive safe-harbor methods (income/net-worth/professional)
- **Income-based verification:** review of specified categories of official income documentation for the relevant look-back period, with a written representation about the current period. (Exact documents, periods, and representations per counsel.)
- **Net-worth-based verification:** review of specified categories of asset documentation and a current consumer credit/liability report, with a written representation about undisclosed liabilities. (Exact documents and dating per counsel.)
- **Third-party-confirmation verification:** written confirmation from a qualifying professional (the categories of licensed/registered professionals counsel recognizes) that they have taken reasonable steps to verify and have determined the purchaser is accredited, within the applicable look-back window.
- **Prior-investor verification:** for a person who previously qualified as an accredited investor in the issuer's prior offering and remains an investor, a written representation that they continue to qualify (use and limits per counsel).

### B.3 Third-party verification services
- Use of a counsel-approved, reputable third-party accredited-investor verification provider that issues verification letters and retains evidence under a defined standard.
- Diligence on the provider (standards, data-handling, audit trail) is itself a counsel-gated step.

### B.4 Professional / entity / status-based categories
- Verification approaches for purchasers qualifying through professional credentials, entity status, or other recognized accredited categories — each with its own evidence pattern, all counsel-specified.

## Part C — Evidence-handling and recordkeeping (framework)
- **Minimize and protect.** Collect only what the chosen method requires; prefer third-party-confirmation methods that avoid the issuer holding sensitive financial documents where counsel agrees it is appropriate.
- **Secure storage** in the access-controlled data room only (cross-ref `REGD-DATAROOM-INDEX.md` §03.4), with view/download logging, encryption, and retention per counsel.
- **Audit trail.** Each verification produces a dated record of the steps taken and the basis for the accredited determination, retained for the period counsel specifies.
- **Non-PHI ring; no co-mingling** with any PHI/patient system. No financial diligence evidence ever touches a PHI runtime.
- **No values in this memo.** All thresholds, look-back periods, and document lists are counsel-specified at execution time.

## Part D — Process flow (prepare-and-flag; nothing live)
1. Counsel retained and method selected → forms and evidence standards approved.
2. Key-rotation hard gate verified GREEN.
3. Verification workflow stood up inside the access-controlled data room (logged, NDA-gated).
4. Each prospective purchaser completes the counsel-approved verification + the Rule 506(d) bad-actor questionnaire (Part E) **before** any subscription is accepted.
5. Counsel reviews edge cases; subscriptions accepted only on a clean accredited determination + a clean bad-actor result.

## Part E — Rule 506(d) Bad-Actor Questionnaire — OUTLINE (counsel finalizes wording)

> **Purpose.** Rule 506(d) disqualifies a Reg D offering from the exemption if a **covered person** has a **disqualifying event.** The issuer must conduct a **factual inquiry** into whether any covered person is subject to a disqualifying event, and document that inquiry. This outline lists the *categories* the questionnaire must cover. **Counsel drafts the operative questions, the certifications, and the disclosure carve-outs (including any pre-existing/waiver/disclosure provisions).**

### E.1 Respondent identification
- Full legal name, role/relationship to the issuer, and the basis on which they are (or are not) a covered person.

### E.2 Covered-person scope (who must complete the questionnaire — counsel confirms the roster)
- The issuer and its predecessors/affiliated issuers.
- Directors, executive officers, other officers participating in the offering, general partners, and managing members.
- Beneficial owners meeting the rule's ownership threshold (threshold per counsel).
- Promoters connected with the issuer.
- Investment managers, solicitors, and compensated persons connected with the sale, and their covered control persons/officers.
- (Cross-ref `REGD-DATAROOM-INDEX.md` §12.4 covered-person roster.)

### E.3 Disqualifying-event categories to inquire into (each as a yes/no with a detail field; counsel sets exact language and look-back periods)
1. **Criminal convictions** in connection with the purchase/sale of a security or involving the making of a false filing or specified financial conduct, within the applicable period.
2. **Court injunctions and restraining orders** related to securities, false filings, or specified financial conduct.
3. **Final orders** of specified regulators (securities, banking, insurance, credit-union, or similar authorities) that bar the person or are based on fraudulent/manipulative/deceptive conduct within the applicable period.
4. **SEC disciplinary orders** under the Exchange Act / Advisers Act (bars, suspensions, specified Section 5 cease-and-desist).
5. **SEC stop orders / Reg A suspension orders** and pending proceedings to issue them.
6. **Suspension or expulsion** from a self-regulatory organization (SRO) for specified conduct.
7. **U.S. Postal Service false-representation orders** within the applicable period.

### E.4 Look-back and dating
- Each category carries the look-back window the rule specifies (counsel inserts the operative periods).
- A representation as to events occurring **before** vs. **on/after** the rule's effective dates, because the consequence differs (disqualification vs. mandatory written disclosure to investors). Counsel sets this language.

### E.5 Disclosure of pre-existing matters
- A field to disclose any matter that occurred **before** the relevant trigger so that counsel can prepare the required written disclosure to investors (a separate obligation from disqualification).

### E.6 Ongoing-update obligation
- An undertaking to promptly notify the issuer of any change in answers, given the obligation to **update the factual inquiry** through the offering period.

### E.7 Certification
- A dated certification that the answers are true and complete, signed by the covered person, retained in the data room per Part C.

### E.8 Waiver / reasonable-care note (counsel-handled)
- Flag for counsel: if a disqualifying event surfaces, counsel evaluates the **reasonable-care exception** (issuer did not know and, exercising reasonable care, could not have known) and/or a **waiver** application. **The Capital seat does not make this call.**
