## 1. The decision in one sentence

Matt: **engage barred securities counsel now** so that, on real disclosable operating traction, the company can run the capital flywheel (the Reg D / Reg CF / reverse-split / disclosure-controls program) on a compliant footing — because **every capital step is gated on counsel and not one of them can proceed without retained counsel first.**

## 2. Why now (and why this is the master unlock)

- The operating engine (the OTCHealth cash machine) is the thing that *legitimizes* any capital action. The Playbook sequence is deliberate: **cash and disclosable traction come first; capital follows.** Counsel needs to be in place **before** the traction arrives so the company is not improvising the capital chain under time pressure.
- The capital flywheel is the single largest value lever in the plan, but it is also the highest-liability lane. **The fastest way to destroy enterprise value is an uncounseled securities action.** Retaining counsel early is the cheapest insurance the company can buy.
- Counsel selects the vehicle, the structure, the accredited-verification method, the sequence, and all filings. **The Capital seat does not choose any of these.** This brief exists only to get the right counsel hired and hand them a clean, organized prepare-and-flag bundle.

## 3. The scope to retain counsel for (the capital chain)

Retain counsel to advise on, structure, and (where applicable) file across this chain. Each item is **framework-level**; counsel sets every specific.

1. **The overall capital chain / sequencing** — what comes first, what is gated on what, and how the operating story and the capital story stay legally separated (Reg FD / MNPI firewall).
2. **Reg D Rule 506(c) (accredited, general-solicitation-permitted) raise** — whether to use it, structure, accredited-investor **reasonable-steps-to-verify** method selection, Form D, blue-sky/state notice filings, and the bad-actor (Rule 506(d)) diligence program.
3. **A Reg CF (Regulation Crowdfunding) portal raise** — whether/when, FINRA-registered funding-portal selection, testing-the-waters rules, the dual-track question (running CF alongside or after 506(c)), and the disclosure package a CF raise requires.
4. **Reverse-split posture** — the corporate-action mechanics and the **FINRA Rule 6490** corporate-action notification process, timing, and prerequisites. (Framework only; no ratios, no share data.)
5. **Pending-litigation disclosure** — how any pending matters must be disclosed in raise materials and in periodic/issuer disclosure, and how that disclosure interacts with the raise timeline.
6. **A disclosure-controls calendar** — standing controls and a calendar for issuer disclosure obligations, Reg FD discipline, an MNPI / insider-information policy, a pre-publish securities-firewall checklist for any externally-facing copy, and a quiet-period / blackout framework around capital actions.

## 4. The types / profiles of counsel to consider (generic profiles, not named firms)

> Engage at least one firm whose **core** practice is securities, not a generalist who dabbles. The differentiators that matter for this company are **OTC / microcap experience** and **small-issuer exempt-offering** depth.

| Profile | Best fit when | Trade-offs |
|---|---|---|
| **Boutique securities firm (small-issuer / exempt-offerings focus)** | Primary recommendation for the near term: Reg D 506(c) + Reg CF + a microcap corporate action. Partner-level attention, cost-efficient, fast, lives in this exact regulatory lane every day. | Smaller bench; confirm capacity for a litigation-disclosure question and for scaling work later. |
| **National / full-service firm with a dedicated securities + capital-markets group** | If the program grows toward larger or institutional capital, a complex corporate action, or M&A roll-up using stock as currency. | Higher cost; partner attention can be diluted; can be heavier than a small exempt-offering needs at this stage. |
| **OTC / microcap-specialist counsel (specifically experienced with OTC-traded issuers, FINRA 6490 corporate actions, and going-/staying-public mechanics)** | **Non-negotiable as either the lead or a co-counsel**, given the public-company vehicle, the reverse-split posture, and the OTC trading venue. This experience is the single most important selection criterion. | A pure microcap shop may be lighter on Reg CF portal mechanics; pair with boutique exempt-offerings depth if so. |
| **Securities-litigation / disclosure counsel (as-needed)** | Brought in or consulted specifically on the **pending-litigation disclosure** question and on disclosure-controls design. | Likely a consult or carve-out, not the lead engagement. |

**Selection criteria to weigh:** depth of actual OTC/microcap issuer work; volume of recent Reg D 506(c) and Reg CF engagements; familiarity with FINRA 6490 corporate actions; bar admission and standing; conflicts check (clean of adverse parties); fee structure (flat-fee per filing vs. hourly); responsiveness; and willingness to act as the standing disclosure-controls advisor, not just a one-off filer.

**Process to recommend to Matt:** shortlist 2–3 firms across the boutique and OTC-specialist profiles, run a conflicts check, request a fixed-scope engagement letter covering §3 items 1–6, and confirm the engagement is signed **before** any capital-chain artifact in the prepare-and-flag bundle leaves draft status.
