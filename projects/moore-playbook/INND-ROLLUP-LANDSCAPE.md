## 1. The thesis (recap) + the honest constraint
INND = the public "Launch Platform." The roll-up lever: acquire complementary hearing /
senior-health businesses, pay primarily in **stock**, and compound enterprise value via
**multiple arbitrage** (buy private revenue cheap, re-rate it at the public multiple) — pairing
with the Playbook's subscription-mix re-rating lever.

**The honest constraint up front:** at ~$0 cash and a thinly-traded sub-penny OTC stock, our
acquisition *currency is weak today.* So the realistic near-term landscape is **small, cheap,
high-strategic-value, low-integration assets** (consumables, a screening/tinnitus app, distressed
IP). The bigger adjacency plays (telehealth, PERS, Rx-savings) are **post-uplisting / post-
capital** — which is exactly why the reverse-split + capital sequence in the flywheel doc come
first. **Uplisting + a healthier multiple is what makes serious roll-up possible.**

## 2. Target-screening scorecard (score each prospect 1–5)
Use this BEFORE anyone is ever contacted. A good target for a capital-light OTC acquirer scores
high here:

| # | Criterion | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Recurring-revenue %** | Subscription/consumables re-rate at ~7–9× rev vs ~2.5–4× for one-time hardware — the engine of the arbitrage. |
| 2 | **Gross-margin quality** | 50%+ (SaaS/membership) funds itself; 35–45% hardware needs scale to move the needle. |
| 3 | **Senior / hearing customer overlap** | Overlap = cross-sell density + shared marketing; unrelated bases add cost, not synergy. |
| 4 | **Profitability / cash burn** | A burning target consumes our working capital or forces dilution. Prefer breakeven+. |
| 5 | **Seller willingness to take stock** | Our currency is illiquid OTC stock — need motivated/strategic sellers (succession, platform thesis). |
| 6 | **Clean cap table + auditable books** | Rule 3-05 may require audited target financials on an 8-K within 75 days; messy books = delay/restatement risk. |
| 7 | **Integration complexity** | Owner-operated DTC/app = fast; multi-site licensed clinical ops = slow, clinician-dependent. |
| 8 | **Regulatory / compliance load** | Medicare/HIPAA (clinics) or FTC/FDA (DTC) — inherited liability risk; prefer clean histories. |
| 9 | **Goodwill / price discipline** | Premium prices pile goodwill; ASC 350 impairment hits EPS if it underperforms. |
| 10 | **Channel + brand fit** | Reinforces (not duplicates) our reach; a target's BBB/FDA blemish can damage a small-cap's credibility. |

## 3. The landscape map (publicly-sourced, illustrative by category)

### A. Hearing vertical
**A1 · DTC / OTC hearing aids & PSAPs** *(direct competitors / consolidation):* LXE Hearing
(Eargo+Lexie, the largest US DTC OTC pure-play), Audien (value tier, 1.5M+ claimed customers),
MDHearing (DTC + embedded screening), Audicus (tele-audiology + health-plan channel), **Nuheara**
(ASX, *in administration 2024 — distressed hearables/OTC IP*). *Fit: customer base + brand;
higher integration; most are bigger than our current currency supports.*

**A2 · Accessories & consumables (the razor-blade layer):** Comply Foam (memory-foam tips),
Cerustop/CeruShield (wax guards), ZPower (rechargeable retrofits), ADCO / HearingAidAccessory.com
(specialty distributors, private-label-able). ***Fit: HIGH — recurring, brand-agnostic, low
integration, directly feeds our consumables-subscription LTV lever. The fragmented category =
the most realistic near-term private-label/roll-up opportunity.***

**A3 · Screening / tinnitus / wellness apps (top-of-funnel):** Mimi (#1 hearing-test app + OEM
personalization API), SonicCloud/Sonitum (Johns Hopkins "Hearing Number"), Oto Health
(RCT-validated tinnitus CBT, subscription). ***Fit: HIGH — cheap, high strategic value as
lead-gen/funnel + a subscription line; complements our own iHEARtest. Note the Soundly acquisition
(Feb 2026) proved buyers pay strategic premiums for top-of-funnel hearing traffic.***

**A4 · Audiology service / tele-audiology networks:** Audicus, Auris Practice Solutions
(remote-audiologist staffing), Neurotone AI/Amptify (aural-rehab — adjacent to our AWARE). *Fit:
recurring service + clinical credibility, BUT clinic networks are clinician-dependent + high
integration — lower priority for a small acquirer.*

### B. Senior-health adjacencies (the "senior-first platform" extension — mostly post-capital)
- **B1 · Senior telehealth:** Teladoc (public), WellBe, Bold Health, CareTalk (partners with device cos). *Cross-sell into the hearing base; bigger, later.*
- **B2 · Rx-savings / pharmacy-discount (our SaveRx concept):** GoodRx (public, Gold $9.99/mo), SingleCare (white-label-able). *Strong cross-sell; partner-or-build more likely than acquire.*

- **B5 · Aging-in-place / supplements / caregiving:** NOVOS ($79/mo longevity), Avidasana (60+ supplements), My Connected Caregiver (RPM bundle). *Subscription cadence matches ours.*

## 5. The multiple-arbitrage model (illustrative round numbers — NOT INND's)
**Setup:** "PublicCo" trades at **5× revenue** on $4M rev (≈$20M illustrative EV). It buys
"PrivateCo" ($2M rev, no recurring) at the private comp of **2.5× rev = $5M.**

**Step 1 — re-rating on close:** $2M of acquired revenue, priced at PublicCo's 5× ≈ **$10M
implied** vs **$5M paid → ~$5M created from the spread alone**, before any synergy.

**Step 2 — subscription uplift:** convert 30% of PrivateCo to recurring (a maintenance/membership
plan). Recurring 30% @ 8× = $4.8M + transactional 70% @ 3.5× = $4.9M → **~$9.7M blended** on the
same $2M base (~4.85× blended) — **~$4.7M of additional implied value from the mix shift.**

**Step 3 — accretion intuition:** a stock deal is accretive when the acquired earnings yield
exceeds the acquirer's implied yield (cheap target + higher-multiple acquirer). **The trap:**
issuing lots of stock at a *depressed* price to fund a deal can dilute away the arbitrage — so
**stock-price discipline + deal sizing are everything for a small-cap OTC acquirer** (the case
for uplisting/reverse-split first).

## 6. Prioritization (how to think about sequence — not a commitment)
- **Tier 1 — realistic near-term (cheap, high-fit, low-integration, cash/small-stock):**
  consumables/private-label (A2) + a screening or tinnitus app (A3) + opportunistic distressed IP
  (e.g. an asset like Nuheara). These directly feed our existing LTV + funnel levers and don't
  need a strong currency.
- **Tier 2 — after first capital / early uplift:** tele-audiology / aural-rehab (A4, ties to
  AWARE) + a small DTC OTC brand (A1) for customer base.
- **Tier 3 — post-uplisting, real currency:** the senior-health adjacencies (B1–B5) — telehealth,
  SaveRx-style Rx savings, PERS/RPM — the platform-expanding, capital-intensive plays.
- **Generally partner-or-build, not acquire (near term):** GoodRx/SingleCare-style Rx savings and
  PERS monitoring are often better as **partnerships/white-label** than acquisitions for a
  capital-light operator.

## 7. Risks specific to a small-/micro-cap OTC roll-up
- **Illiquid stock = weak currency** (thin volume; sellers demand more shares for liquidity risk).
- **Rule 3-05 disclosure burden** (audited target financials on an 8-K; small targets rarely have them).
- **Goodwill impairment** (ASC 350) if an acquisition underperforms — can wipe out quarters of EPS.
- **Dilution spiral** — stock-funded deals that don't earn out depress price → each next deal needs more shares.
- **Integration risk** — in hearing, loyalty follows the clinician, not the brand; simultaneous integrations on a thin team degrade quality everywhere.
