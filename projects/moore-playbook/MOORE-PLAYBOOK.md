## 0. The one-paragraph thesis

The Moore family has spent three generations and 80 years putting hearing within reach of
ordinary people. We now hold the rarest combination in business: a **proven product**, a
**warm 85,000-person customer base**, a **public-company vehicle**, and — for the first time
in history — an **AI workforce that lets one operator run the company of a thousand.** The
Moore Playbook is the plan to fuse two engines into one self-reinforcing machine: **OTCHealth**
(the cash engine — a Medvi-style marketing-and-distribution layer on hearing products we
already own) and **InnerScope/INND** (the capital flywheel — a public-company "Launch
Platform" that turns operating wins into capital, acquisition currency, and narrative, which
funds more operating wins). Cash proves the story; the story raises capital; capital buys more
cash. Compounded, that loop is how a solo operator reaches a billion-dollar company.

## 3. The Moore Flywheel (the core idea)

Two engines, one loop. Each turn makes the next turn easier.

```
        OTCHealth (CASH ENGINE)                         InnerScope / INND (CAPITAL FLYWHEEL)
   ┌──────────────────────────────┐                 ┌────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ reignite list → TReO sales   │   traction →    │ disclosable IR narrative +          │
   │ → OTC line → subscriptions   │ ─────────────►  │ shareholder activation              │
   │ → app portfolio revenue      │                 │ → public capital (Reg D/CF/A+)      │
   └──────────────────────────────┘                 │ → stock as acquisition currency     │
              ▲                                      └────────────────────────────────────┘
              │  fund inventory, FDA reg, ads,                      │
              │  app builds, M&A roll-ups          ◄────────────────┘
              └──────────────────────  capital + currency  ─────────────────────
```

**The loop in one line:** Operating traction → a legitimate public-company story → public
capital + acquisition currency → fund more inventory / FDA registration / paid growth / app
builds / acquisitions → more operating traction → repeat, at a larger radius each time.

**Why the public company is an accelerant, not a burden** (structural advantages a private
company cannot replicate):
1. **Capital at multiple tiers** — Reg D 506(c) (accredited, live), Reg CF/WeFunder (retail),
   Reg A+ (larger) — run sequentially as the story grows.
2. **Acquisition currency** — buy complementary brands/distribution with stock, not cash. The
   classic roll-up: acquire at a low revenue multiple while the public vehicle trades at a
   higher one — instantly accretive to enterprise value, no operating cash spent.
3. **Credibility + an owned audience** — an existing shareholder base is a self-selected
   audience that compounds the story at zero marginal cost (Reg-FD-compliant comms only).

> **Hard firewall (non-negotiable):** the two engines are run **strictly separate in
> execution.** No MNPI, securities, or INND/IR content moves without Matt + counsel. The
> flywheel is a strategy, not a license to blur the wall.

We don't need the whole market. **Capturing a few points of a growing multi-billion-dollar
category, at 85-90% wedge margins, with an owned recurring back-end, is a billion-dollar
enterprise.**

## 7. The math to $1B (illustrative model — assumptions, not promises)

Value = **revenue × margin × multiple**, amplified by the public vehicle. The path is not one
hero product; it's a **recurring base** plus **ascension** plus **portfolio** plus **roll-up**.

**The load-bearing lever is recurring subscribers.** ~**200,000 members × ~$19.99/mo ≈ ~$480M
ARR** — that single line is roughly half of a billion-dollar revenue base, and Medvi proved a
lean team can reach that subscriber scale in health DTC. The other halves come from device
revenue (TReO + OTC line + the 10,298-unit pool), consumables replenishment, the app
portfolio, and acquired revenue.

**The three fastest compounders (in priority order):**
1. **Churn** — every 1% monthly churn improvement at 50K subs adds ~$9M in LTV. Retention >
   acquisition.
2. **Subscription mix** — markets value recurring revenue at ~4-6× vs ~1-1.5× for hardware.
   The *same* $200M ARR is worth ~$300M as a device business or ~$1B+ as a subscription
   business. **Mix is the valuation lever.**
3. **M&A roll-up arbitrage** — the public vehicle can acquire complementary revenue at a low
   multiple and have it re-rated at the company's higher multiple — enterprise value created
   without operating cash.

**Scenarios:**
- **Base:** reignite → device + early subscriptions → low-eight-figure revenue; healthy,
  default-alive, funds itself.
- **Bull:** subscription engine + OTC ascension + paid scale → nine-figure revenue, the Medvi
  trajectory.
- **The $1B path:** the bull engine **+** subscription-mix re-rating **+** pubco-funded roll-up
  of adjacent hearing/senior-health brands. All three, compounding, over multiple years.

**Where the model is fragile:** every line is downstream of **Phase 1 — does the warm list
still convert?** Nothing else matters until that's proven. (See §8, Weeks 1-2.)

## 9. The moat (the throughline that protects all of it)
**Compliance enforced in code.** Every claim — owned AND affiliate — passes `claims_check`
before it ships (FTC holds the brand liable for affiliate claims; ads/advertorials screened
hardest — Medvi's exact failure point). TReO is a PSAP, never a hearing-aid/medical/FDA claim.
Market only what ships today. PHI never touches the non-BAA runtime. The securities firewall is
absolute. **This is why we win where Medvi blew up.**
