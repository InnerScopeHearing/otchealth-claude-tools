# Chapter 14 Workbook — Saving the Return

---

## The Takeaway

When a patient comes in holding that white envelope, they are not returning a device — they are telling you they have lost faith in the journey. Your job in that moment is not to defend the product or cite the fitting rationale. It is to listen until you find the one real issue driving them toward the door, address that single issue specifically, and reconnect them to why they started. The patient who walks back out with their hearing aids is the one who got a person genuinely invested in keeping them in the journey.

> Closer lens: A return in your office is still a live opportunity. The patient came back, which means they have not given up — they are giving you one more chance. Listen, find the one objection, re-close on you and the promise you made them.
> Professional lens: The save is an act of care. When you sit with a frustrated patient and hear them fully before you say a word in defense of anything, you are demonstrating the kind of commitment that changes a skeptic into someone who stays.
