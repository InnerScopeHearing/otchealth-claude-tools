## Chapter 1 Workbook — Two Chairs, One Family

---

### 1. The Takeaway

This business runs on relationships that begin long before anyone discusses a hearing aid. The support role — the front desk, the phone call, the warm room — is not background work; it is the foundation everything else stands on. Knowing *why* you are in this work, and *which role is yours to own*, is how you show up with the consistency patients can feel the moment they walk through your door.

### 4. The Drill — Your Role Statement

**What it is:** A two-sentence statement you can say aloud — to a new team member, to yourself on a hard day, to a patient who asks how long you've been doing this — that captures your role and your reason for it.

**The shape (from the manuscript):**
> "The front desk person controls the environment, the flow of the business, and the outcome of how many hearing aids are going to get sold that day." — Kim Moore

That is a role statement with conviction. Yours should have the same conviction — in your words, for your role.

**Write your Role Statement:**

My role in this practice is __________

__________

I do it because __________

__________

When I do my role well, the patient feels __________

__________

**Now read it aloud.** If it sounds like you, keep it. If it sounds like a job description, rewrite it until it sounds like you.
