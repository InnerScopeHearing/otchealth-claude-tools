## Chapter 6 Workbook — What Brings You In Today?

---

### The Takeaway

Nobody comes in to buy a hearing aid. Every person who sits across from you came for a more complicated reason — and your job is to find that reason before you say a word about a device. The four-word opening question, and the discipline of repeating back exactly what the patient says, does more to build trust and help them feel genuinely heard than any presentation ever could. Ask first. Listen second. Repeat it back. Then — and only then — begin.

### The Drill — The Repeat-Back Script Builder

The repeat-back technique has three moves: (1) ask the open question, (2) repeat back what they say without edits or spin, (3) let the silence do the work until they say more. Build your version below.
