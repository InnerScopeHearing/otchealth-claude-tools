# From the Chair — STYLE & LEXICON (master consistency doc)

Every writer (human or Sonnet subagent) follows this. It is what keeps 18 chapters + 2 editions in one voice.

## 1. What we're writing
ONE shared-core manuscript, written edition-neutral, that Phase 3 splits into two editions via the lexicon below:
- EDITION A — "The Closer" (owners / salespeople / commission dispensers; sales-oriented).
- EDITION B — "The Professional" (front desk / support staff / dispensers who lead with care, not sales).
The book is co-authored "Mark & Kim Moore." Each chapter has a PRIMARY VOICE (Mark or Kim) per the spec sheet.

## 2. Voice rules
- Write in the primary author's FIRST PERSON ("I"), faithful to their actual interview voice — lightly edited
  transcript cadence, warm and plainspoken, NOT corporate. Keep their real phrasing and stories.
- Use verbatim quotes (pulled from the transcripts) as occasional pull-quotes — format: a line starting with ">".
- Where the OTHER Moore has complementary material, add a short sidebar titled "**From the other chair**"
  (1 short paragraph) in their voice. This is how both show up in every chapter.
- Mark = direct, story-driven, sales-as-service, a little wry. Kim = warm, caring, detail-loving, front-desk pride.
- NEVER invent facts, patients, stats, or quotes. Only use what's in the transcripts/spines. If unsure, leave it out.

## 3. Per-chapter structure (shared core, edition-neutral)
1. Chapter title (from spec) + primary-voice byline tag, e.g. *(Mark)*.
2. A 1-line epigraph: a real verbatim quote from that chapter's source.
3. The narrative (~1,600–2,200 words): the story + the method, in the author's voice.
4. "From the other chair" sidebar (~80–130 words) — the other Moore's complementary angle.
5. "The principle" — 2–4 sentences distilling the teachable point.
6. "The AI-Layer" — 2–4 sentences bridging the timeless principle to how it scales with today's AI tools
   (lifecycle follow-up, AI front desk, modality-matched messaging, etc.). Honest, not hype.
7. Two short edition-lens callouts at the very end (these seed Phase 3):
   - "**For the Closer**" (1–2 sentences, sales framing)
   - "**For the Professional**" (1–2 sentences, care framing)
- Output as Markdown.

## 4. The LEXICON (same technique, two vocabularies — Phase 3 swap)
| Shared concept | Edition A (Closer) | Edition B (Professional) |
|---|---|---|
| build trust before recommending | sell yourself, not the device | earn the patient's trust first |
| surface objections early | handle objections before they kill the sale | understand the patient's real concerns |
| denial>stigma>vanity>cost | what's really blocking the sale | what's really keeping them from care |
| Thinker/Knower/Feeler | read the buyer, close the buyer | communicate the way each patient needs |
| ask for the decision | ask for the sale | help them say yes to better hearing |
| outcomes (COSI 3 goals) | lock the win, drive referrals | define & deliver patient success |
| the metric | close rate · revenue · commission | help/fit-success rate · outcomes · satisfaction |
| the reader's role | the closer / the owner | the professional / the caregiver |
Writers: keep the shared core neutral; only the two end callouts + a few flagged phrases differ. Phase 3 applies the swap fully.

## 5. Compliance (non-negotiable — bake in while writing)
- Mark & Kim are **Licensed Hearing Aid Dispensers** — NEVER "audiologist"/"Au.D." for them. (General advice to "see a licensed professional" is fine.)
- This is a memoir/method about TRADITIONAL hearing aids. Do NOT mention or imply the iHEAR TReO PSAP; no cross-promotion.
- No medical/treatment/cure claims. Mark's health statements (e.g., hearing-loss/cognitive links) are HIS
  experience — frame as such; a global "general education, not medical advice; consult a licensed professional" note ships in the front matter.
- INND is a public company — keep heritage/track-record factual and modest; no securities/financial claims.
