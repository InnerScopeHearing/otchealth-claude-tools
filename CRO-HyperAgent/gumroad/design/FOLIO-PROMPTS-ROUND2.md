## Read me first - what changed and why

THE FIVE GLOBAL DECISIONS (apply across everything):
1. GO DARK and cinematic for all three covers (the v2 direction wins). The light/cream covers read like a brochure; the dark single-chair-in-warm-light reads like a premium trade book and survives as a tiny LinkedIn thumbnail. Amber-lit for A, teal-lit for B, two-chairs for the bundle. The matched dark set is what makes it look like a SERIES and builds reputation.
2. FIX THE COVER LOCKUP. Stop floating the title in a flat top band with a hairline rule (it reads like a newsletter masthead). Integrate the title into the image space, give the subtitle real hierarchy, and anchor the byline with a CREDIBILITY LINE (three generations, 40,000+ patients). That authority cue is what converts skeptical professionals.
3. ADOPT THE v3 'THREE PATIENTS' CARDS as the hero modality visual (far more premium than the donut wheel) and FIX the raw HTML entity bug (curly-quote entities must render as real glyphs everywhere).
4. TIGHTEN LEGIBILITY on all diagrams: no clipped nodes at canvas edges, no tiny low-contrast cue text.
5. PRODUCE THE MISSING PIECES: 3D mockups, Gumroad listing graphics, and a full LinkedIn launch kit (quote cards, carousel, teaser). These are what Matt actually pushes to his network.

ENCODING NOTE: every binary asset in this repo (Folio art and the heritage photos) is stored as base64 text inside the file (github push stores content verbatim). To use any image, run base64 -d file.png > real.png once. This is the working convention across all repo art. Compliance unchanged: Licensed Hearing Aid Dispensers (never 'audiologist'), no device/medical/FDA claims, no iHEAR TReO/PSAP, do not depict a hearing aid.

PHOTOS IN: the three real Moore-family heritage photos are in the repo at design/photos/ (marvin-posey-1950s.png, mark-testing-mother-1980s.png, moore-family-1989.png) with a README. Prompts R9, R10, R11 use them. A current present-day photo will follow as design/photos/moore-family-present.png.
