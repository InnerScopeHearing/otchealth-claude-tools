# From the Chair — Brand Brief (for Folio)

Read this before producing any asset. All visuals share ONE identity; the two editions differ only by accent color.

## Project
"From the Chair" — a professional book + fillable workbook system built from the Moore family's real
hearing-care career (3 generations). It sells to hearing-practice professionals in TWO editions of the
same content:
- EDITION A — "The Closer" (owners / salespeople / commission dispensers; sales-oriented).
- EDITION B — "The Professional" (front desk / call center / support staff / dispensers who lead with care, not sales).

## Mood
Warm, literary, trustworthy, multi-generational, human. Premium but approachable. The feeling of a
worn leather chair in a family practice where someone's life is about to get better — NOT a cold clinical office.

## Palette
- Shared: paper cream #F6EFE3 (and #FFFDF8), ink #2A2521, gold accent #B8862F, hairline #E4DAC9.
- Edition A (Closer): primary warm amber-brown #8A4B2F, soft #BF7E54. Confident, energetic.
- Edition B (Professional): primary teal-green #3F7A6D, soft #5B9B8C. Calm, professional, warm.
- The Bundle: balanced use of both accents (amber + teal) on cream.

## Typography
- Display / titles: Fraunces (literary serif, the brand face).
- Body / labels / UI: Inter.

## Motif
The CHAIR — the testing/fitting chair and the front-desk chair. A single empty chair in warm light
("someone's story begins here") is the signature image; "two chairs" is the recurring idea (sales chair + care chair).
Secondary motifs: sound/hearing rendered warmly (gentle arcs, never clinical audiogram-cold); the family-practice lobby (coffee, warmth).

## Hard avoids
- Cold clinical white/blue "medical" look; sterile hospital imagery.
- Generic AI-art clichés: glowing orbs, particles, circuitry, neon.
- Stock "doctor/audiologist" photos; depicting the iHEAR TReO product.
- Any text/visual implying "audiologist," "FDA," "medical device," or that a device treats/cures hearing loss.
  (Mark & Kim are Licensed Hearing Aid Dispensers — never "audiologist." This is a memoir/method, not a medical claim.)
- Casino/hard-sell/gimmick aesthetics.
