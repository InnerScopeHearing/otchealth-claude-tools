# From the Chair — EDITION SWAP SPEC (Phase 3)

Turn the edition-NEUTRAL shared core into the two sellable editions. SAME content, SAME stories, SAME page
count — only the framing vocabulary and the end callouts differ. This is the repackaging that lets us sell two
products from one manuscript.

- EDITION A — "The Closer" — owners / salespeople / commission dispensers. Sales-oriented, confident, results.
- EDITION B — "The Professional" — front desk / call center / support staff / dispensers who lead with care, not
  sales. Calm, care-first, never pushy. (Includes dispensers who don't want to "talk money.")

## Inputs (read first, every writer)
- /tmp/ftc/inputs/STYLE-AND-LEXICON.md  — §4 the LEXICON table is your swap dictionary. Memorize it.
- The neutral source files you're assigned (manuscript /tmp/ftc/manuscript/chNN.md OR workbook /tmp/ftc/workbook/wbNN.md).

## The swap rules (apply ALL)
1. **End callouts.**
   - Edition A: KEEP the `**For the Closer**` callout. DELETE the `**For the Professional**` callout entirely.
   - Edition B: KEEP the `**For the Professional**` callout. DELETE the `**For the Closer**` callout entirely.
2. **Lens-flagged lines (workbook).** Lines flagged `> Closer lens:` / `> Professional lens:`:
   - Edition A: replace the neutral statement with the Closer-lens wording; delete BOTH flag lines (the `>` lines).
   - Edition B: replace the neutral statement with the Professional-lens wording; delete BOTH flag lines.
   - (Net effect: the reader sees one clean sentence in their own vocabulary, no visible "lens" scaffolding.)
3. **Lexicon vocabulary (body text).** Where the neutral text uses a concept in the lexicon table, shift the
   FRAMING to the edition's column — but ONLY where it reads naturally. Examples (from STYLE-AND-LEXICON §4):
   - "ask for the decision" → A: "ask for the sale" · B: "help them say yes to better hearing"
   - "build trust before recommending" → A: "sell yourself, not the device" · B: "earn the patient's trust first"
   - "surface objections early" → A: "handle objections before they kill the sale" · B: "understand the patient's real concerns"
   - the metric → A: close rate / revenue / commission · B: help-success / outcomes / satisfaction
   - the reader's role → A: the closer / the owner · B: the professional / the caregiver
   DO NOT rewrite stories, quotes, the epigraph, the "From the other chair" sidebar, "The principle", or
   "The AI-Layer" — those stay shared/neutral. Only adjust framing sentences in the narrative + section intros.
4. **Tone dial, light touch.** A = a little more drive/closing energy; B = a little more warmth/care. Achieve this
   through the lexicon swaps and callout, NOT by adding or cutting paragraphs. Keep length within ±5% of the source.
5. **NEVER** change facts, patient stories, names, numbers, quotes, or add medical/treatment/cure language.
   Compliance unchanged: Licensed Hearing Aid Dispensers (never "audiologist"); traditional hearing aids only;
   no iHEAR TReO / PSAP.
