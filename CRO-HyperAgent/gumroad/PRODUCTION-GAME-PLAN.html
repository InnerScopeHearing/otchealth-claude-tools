The Workbook Strategy — the core of your question

Why the workbook IS the product

At a real price point, the book is the story and the workbook is the value. Two reasons to make it heavy:

  1. Value & engagement: people pay for transformation, not pages. A workbook that makes them apply each principle to their own practice, their own patients, their own numbers is what justifies a professional price — and what actually changes their results, which drives reviews, referrals, and repeat buyers.
  2. Anti-piracy moat: a blank PDF can be shared — but it's nearly worthless blank. The value only exists once filled in with the reader's real data, which makes it personal, effortful, and pointless to copy. A finished workbook becomes their operating manual, not a file to pass around. We reinforce this with per-buyer personalization (name + license # on the cover, a Gumroad license key, a light watermark).

The exercise toolkit (mixed into every chapter)

Each chapter's workbook draws from this set so it's varied, active, and substantial — not blank lines:

  • Self-Score — rate yourself 1–10 on the chapter's skill (baseline → re-score at 30/60/90 days)
  • Script Builder — write your words for the technique (your repeat-back, your cost-reframe)
  • Real-Patient Log — track 3–5 actual patients applying the principle, and the outcome
  • This-Week Drill — a specific action to run this week + what happened
  • Decision Tool — a worksheet they complete (modality identifier, COSI 3-goals capture, objection map)
  • Metric Tracker — baseline → target → actual on the number that matters
  • Role-Play Card — practice with a colleague + a scored checklist
  • Reflect & Commit — a personal insight + a dated commitment with an accountability partner

The per-chapter template (consistent spine, ~3–5 pages of work each)

  1. The Principle — one line + the one quote from Mark/Kim
  2. Where I Am Now — self-score + a short honest diagnostic
  3. Make It Mine — the script/decision tool, filled in their words
  4. Try It This Week — the drill + the real-patient log
  5. Measure It — the metric tracker
  6. Reflect & Commit — insight + dated commitment

Across ~18 chapters that's ~70–110 pages of genuine, fillable work — a real implementation system, not an afterthought. Same template in both editions; the prompts get the vocabulary swap.
