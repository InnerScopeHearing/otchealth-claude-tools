# PART ONE: THE IMPLEMENTATION TRACKER

## What This Is

The 90-day tracker is for the practice owner or manager rolling the *From the Chair* method out to a team. It maps the 18 chapters across 9 Parts to a weekly cadence, assigns a team drill each week, and gives you a clear ownership column and a done-check. The scorecard lets you measure where the practice stands at the start, at the 30-day mark, at 60 days, and at 90 days — and gives your team a concrete target to work toward.

**A note on the scorecard metrics:** The metric labels are intentionally neutral so they work whether your practice measures success through a sales lens or a care lens. Fill in the numbers that are meaningful to you and your team.

---

## 90-Day Chapter Rollout Table

**Instructions:** One chapter per week. Assign an owner (manager, lead dispenser, front desk lead, or "whole team"). Run the listed drill at your weekly team huddle or dedicated training session. Check the Done column when the team has completed the reading AND the drill.

# PART TWO: MASTER INDEX — HOW IT ALL FITS TOGETHER

## What's in the Bundle

The *From the Chair* product bundle is a complete practice development system built from the real careers of Mark & Kim Moore, Licensed Hearing Aid Dispensers — 23+ years, 40,000+ patients, one of the largest privately owned hearing aid practices in the country. Everything below is drawn from the same source: their actual experience, in their actual voices.

Here is every component, what it is, and how to use it.

---

### The Books

---

**Edition A — *From the Chair: The Closer***

*What it is:* The full 18-chapter memoir-and-method, written in Mark and Kim's voices, filtered through a sales and business-growth lens. Every chapter includes the real story, the teachable principle, and a "For the Closer" callout that frames the method in terms of outcomes, close rates, patient conversion, and practice revenue. Written for practice owners, lead dispensers, and anyone on the team who is responsible for results.

*How to use it:* Read cover to cover once — do not skip chapters. The method builds. Then return to individual chapters when you face a specific challenge (a patient stuck in denial, a difficult return, a team culture problem). Keep it at your desk. Dog-ear the chapters that hit hardest.

---

**Edition B — *From the Chair: The Professional***

*What it is:* The same 18-chapter book, same stories, same principles — re-framed through a care and professional development lens. Every "For the Professional" callout centers patient outcomes, trust-building, honest expectations, and the satisfaction of doing the work well. Written for front desk staff, support team members, newer dispensers, and anyone who leads with care rather than commission.

*How to use it:* The same way — read cover to cover first. Then return to chapters that speak to your role. Chapters 5, 6, and 9 are especially foundational for anyone at the front desk. Chapters 3, 4, and 18 are foundational for anyone who wants to understand why this work matters.

---

### The Workbook

---

**The Fillable Workbook — 18 Modules**

*What it is:* One module per chapter. Each module gives you the chapter's core principle in a single sentence, a reflection prompt, a skill-building exercise (the Mirror Drill, the repeat-back, modality-tagging, the loved-one demo script, the return-save protocol, the COSI goals capture, and more), and a patient log page. The workbook opens with a "Your Why" module and closes with a 30/60/90 re-score page that mirrors the scorecard in this tracker.

*How to use it:* Fill it while you read, chapter by chapter. This is not a test — it is a thinking tool. Your answers are for you. If you are a manager using it in 1:1s, the self-scores and patient log entries are coaching prompts, not surveillance. Use them to ask questions, not to grade.

---

### The Reference Tools

---

**The Scripts Library**

*What it is:* The complete script bank, organized by the patient journey — from first phone contact through the fitting, the adaptation period, the return-save, and the recall/referral ask. 18–25 individual scripts, each with a "when to use it" note, the Moores' real phrasing, and a "my version" blank line so you can adapt it to your own voice. Where phrasing differs by role, both the Closer and Professional versions are shown, labeled clearly.

*How to use it:* After reading the relevant book chapter, come here to find the actual words. Practice the line out loud before you need it in the room. The scripts are not meant to be read verbatim — they are meant to be internalized until they sound like you.

---

**The Quick-Reference Cards**

*What it is:* Six self-contained, print-ready cards designed to lie flat on a desk or be pinned to a wall. Each card covers one critical skill area: (1) The Modality Wheel — Thinker/Knower/Feeler with communication cues; (2) The Objection Iceberg — the four objections in their real order with one-line reframes; (3) The Patient Journey — every stage from first call to referral, one cue per step; (4) The First-Contact Card — the phone greeting, the opener, the repeat-back; (5) The Close Card — cost-of-inaction, the one-price-includes-me line, both editions labeled; (6) The Return-Save Card — the white-envelope signal, the one-issue protocol.

*How to use it:* Print them and keep them where you work. Glance at the relevant card before a difficult conversation. Use them in team drills — cover the "what to say" column and have the team supply it from memory.

---

**The Team Rollout / Training Kit**

*What it is:* A standalone manager's resource for bringing the method to a full team. Includes a 4-week onboarding plan (reading + workbook modules + drills), 6–8 role-play scenarios drawn directly from real situations in the book (the refund daughter, the British-PBS return, the engineer who'd build his own, the denial patient, the white-envelope return, the nervous first-timer), a weekly drill calendar, and a short manager's guide for using filled workbooks in 1:1s.

*How to use it:* Read this before you begin the 90-day rollout in this tracker. The training kit gives you the detailed scenario scripts and drill formats that the rollout table above references. They work together — this tracker gives you the week-by-week roadmap; the training kit gives you the content to run in each session.

---

**The Companion Audio Script**

*What it is:* A narrated script for an audio companion — a listen-in-the-car version of the book. Warm, spoken-word register. One 2–3 minute spoken segment per Part (9 Parts), each anchored to the real story from that Part and the core takeaway, in Mark's or Kim's voice as labeled. Opens with a brief introduction to who Mark and Kim are and the three-generation heritage of the practice. Closes with the same letter to the reader that ends the book.

*How to use it:* Listen between sessions, during commutes, or when you want to revisit a principle without re-reading. This is reinforcement, not a substitute for the book — the audio hits the heart; the book gives you the method. Use both.

---

**This Document — The Implementation Tracker + Master Index**

*What it is:* The front-door document for the entire bundle. The 90-day rollout table, the practice scorecard, and the component guide are all here.

*How to use it:* Start here. Read Part Two (this index) first to orient yourself to all the components. Then turn to the 90-day tracker, fill in your starting scorecard numbers, assign the Week 1 owner, and begin.

**In plain language:**

1. **Read the book** — your edition, cover to cover. Let the stories land before you start applying the method.
2. **Fill the workbook** — chapter by chapter, alongside or just after the book. This is where the method becomes yours.
3. **Drill with the cards** — once you've read and reflected, the quick-reference cards let you practice and self-check in real time.
4. **Roll it out to the team** — use the Training Kit for scenario-based team sessions and the 90-day tracker in this document to keep the rollout on schedule.
5. **Reinforce with audio** — use the companion audio script (when recorded) to revisit the principles between reading sessions, on commutes, and as ongoing team culture reinforcement.

## A Note on Both Editions

If you are an owner or manager, consider providing both editions to your team — Edition A to the dispensers and anyone tracking outcomes, Edition B to the front desk and support staff. The stories and principles are identical. What changes is the vocabulary and the lens. A team that has read both tends to communicate better across roles, because they understand how their colleagues are framing the same work.
