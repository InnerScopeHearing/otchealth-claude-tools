From the Chair

Three generations of caring for people who couldn't hear — the Moore family method, told from two chairs.

One book · two editions (Patients & Families · Hearing Professionals) · eBook + fillable Workbook · an AI-Layer bridge in every chapter.
Built from Mark Moore's 3-part interview (sales & ownership) + Kim Moore's interview (care & front desk). Both Licensed Hearing Aid Dispensers.

The Concept

Two chairs, one family

Every patient in the Moores' world sat in two chairs: the front-desk and testing chair, where Kim made them feel safe and learned who they were — and the sales chair, where Mark earned their trust and helped them decide. From the Chair is the wisdom from both, woven into one narrative: part memoir, part method, all heart. The thesis that unites them — you don't sell a hearing aid, you help a person; people don't want to be sold, they want to be cared for.

Why two editions of one book: the stories and the spine are identical — only the framing changes. The Patients & Families edition reads as reassurance and a roadmap ("here's what the journey feels like, and how to help someone you love"). The Hearing Professionals edition reads as a teachable system ("here's how to run a caring, profitable practice") and carries the Workbook exercises. Write once; frame twice.

The Method, Combined

The Moore Method

The spine that runs beneath the stories — Mark's selling-as-serving and Kim's care-as-system.

Mark — Selling Is Serving

  1. You sell yourself, not the device — the aid is just the tool
  2. People don't want to be sold; they want to buy (and to be helped)
  3. The four objections, inverted: denial → stigma → vanity → cost
  4. Read the person: Thinkers / Knowers / Feelers — match their modality
  5. "What brings you in today?" + repeat-back to draw out the real reason
  6. Price = the device plus me; cost of inaction; today is the best day
  7. A return isn't a returned aid — it's the patient returning you
  8. The duplicatable patient journey process + modified COSI (3 listening goals)

Kim — Care Is the System

  1. The caring starts on the first phone call, long before any sale
  2. You are the business — smile before you pick up the phone
  3. You hear with your brain, not your ears; give the month, hold the hand
  4. Rituals that signal care: the Hershey bar, the gold stickers
  5. The loved-one demo turns a skeptical family into allies
  6. Saving a return: find the one issue that actually matters
  7. Hire for heart: "What's your relationship with your parents/grandparents?"
  8. Treat every patient like your own family — and you're never pushy
