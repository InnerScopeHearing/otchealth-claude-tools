# SOP-05: Return and Refund Policy for OTC Medical Devices
### FTC and State Law Compliant Template

---

**PRODUCT TITLE (Gumroad listing):**
Return and Refund Policy SOP for OTC Medical Devices — FTC and State Law Compliant Template

**GUMROAD LISTING DESCRIPTION (copy-paste ready):**
OTC medical devices occupy an awkward legal space for returns: they look like retail
consumer goods but carry FDA device obligations that complicate your refund policy.
Accept a return without the right documentation and you may restock a device that
should have been quarantined. Refuse a return without a disclosed policy and you face
FTC and state consumer protection exposure.

This SOP gives you a legally grounded, customer-friendly return and refund policy
for OTC devices, including the hygiene exception language that protects you from
having to resell returned ear-contact devices, plus the complaint routing you need
to catch safety events before they become MDRs.

Designed for OTC device brands selling direct-to-consumer (website, Amazon) and
for pharmacy and retail operations carrying OTC devices on the floor.

What you get: a complete policy template with FTC-compliant disclosure language,
a state-law variation flag list (30-day rules, restocking fee restrictions, digital
delivery rules), the hygiene exception framework, a returned product disposition
decision tree, and a customer-facing policy page draft you can drop into your website
or print for in-store posting.

Covers both physical device returns and digital/software component refunds.

## 4. RETURN INTAKE PROCEDURE

### 4.1 Receiving a Return Request

When a customer contacts us to initiate a return:

1. Confirm the order number and purchase date against your order management system.
2. Confirm the product is within the return window.
3. Ask the customer for the reason for the return. Record the reason verbatim. This
   is important: if the stated reason suggests a safety concern, malfunction, or
   injury, the return must be routed to SOP-03 (Complaint Handling) in addition to
   being processed as a return.
4. Assess whether the hygiene exception applies. If the customer confirms the seal
   was removed and the device was ear-contact, apply the hygiene exception. Explain
   the policy clearly and offer alternatives (troubleshooting, exchange, discount
   on next purchase) if appropriate.
5. Issue a Return Merchandise Authorization (RMA) number. Log the RMA in your
   order management system.
6. Email the customer return instructions and, where applicable, a prepaid shipping
   label within 2 business days.

### 4.2 Safety Triage at Intake

During intake, watch for these signals that require routing to SOP-03 before
processing the return:

- Customer mentions injury, pain, hearing change, skin irritation, or medical visit
- Customer describes a malfunction (sparking, overheating, unexpected sounds,
  component breaking)
- Customer mentions the device was involved in a fall or accident
- Any concern about harm to a person

If any of these are present: flag the return as a safety return. Notify the
regulatory coordinator within 4 hours. Do not process the return as routine until
the regulatory coordinator confirms MDR assessment is complete (or not required).

## 5. RETURNED PRODUCT DISPOSITION

### 5.1 Receiving a Returned Device

Upon physical receipt of a returned device:

1. Log receipt date and RMA number.
2. Inspect the outer packaging. Document condition (photos recommended).
3. Classify the returned unit per the decision tree below.

### 5.2 Returned Product Decision Tree

STEP 1: Was this return flagged as a safety return (per Section 4.2)?
- If YES: quarantine the device. Do not open or test. Preserve as-received for
  regulatory coordinator review. Proceed to STEP 3 only after regulatory coordinator
  releases the unit.
- If NO: proceed to STEP 2.

STEP 2: Is the device in resalable condition?
- Outer packaging intact and undamaged
- Hygienic seal intact (for ear-contact devices)
- All accessories and documentation present
- No evidence of use (no ear wax, skin contact residue, or odor)
If ALL of the above: RESALABLE. Return to inventory.
If ANY item fails: proceed to STEP 3.

STEP 3: Is the device safe to test and potentially refurbish?
- No safety concern flagged
- No evidence of damage or misuse
If YES: route to product testing / refurbishment process (if your company has one).
If NO: QUARANTINE for destruction or manufacturer return.

STEP 4: Destruction or manufacturer return.
- Log the unit on the Returned Product Disposition Log (Form LOG-RET-01).
- Process destruction or manufacturer return credit per your distributor agreement.

### 5.3 Hygiene Exception Handling

Returned ear-contact devices with a removed hygienic seal cannot be restocked for
resale. Options:
- Return to manufacturer for credit (check distributor/manufacturer return agreement)
- Destruction with documentation
- Use as demo/display unit with clear "Display Only" labeling

Document disposition on Form LOG-RET-01.

## 6. DIGITAL PRODUCTS AND SOFTWARE LICENSES

If your OTC device includes a companion app or software license:

- Software licenses are generally non-refundable once activated or downloaded.
- State your digital non-refund policy on the checkout page and in the product
  description before purchase.
- Exception: if the software is defective and customer support cannot resolve the
  issue within [7] business days, issue a full refund.
- FTC guidance: "all sales final" policies for digital goods are permitted but must
  be clearly disclosed before purchase, not after.

## 7. STATE LAW VARIATIONS (COMPLETE BEFORE PUBLISHING YOUR POLICY)

Several states impose consumer protection requirements that override or supplement
your stated return policy. Key variations to check:

| State | Rule |
|-------|------|
| California | No explicit mandatory return window, but "all sales final" must be posted conspicuously at POS. Prohibits certain gift card sales-final policies. |
| Connecticut | Retailers with a return policy must post it conspicuously. No mandatory window. |
| Florida | No mandatory return window. Clear disclosure is sufficient. |
| Massachusetts | Retailers must post return policy at POS. If none posted, customer may return for full refund within a reasonable time. |
| New York | Retailers with a limited or no-return policy must post it prominently. If no policy posted, must accept returns within 30 days. |
| New Jersey | Return policy must be posted. If not posted, must accept returns within 20 days. |
| Virginia | Must accept returns within 20 days if no policy posted. |

Note on restocking fees: some states restrict restocking fees on returned goods.
California, for example, limits restocking fees and requires disclosure. Verify
your state's rules before applying a restocking fee.

## 8. AMAZON AND THIRD-PARTY MARKETPLACE RETURNS

If you sell through Amazon FBA or FBM, Amazon's return policy governs returns made
through the Amazon channel, not your DTC policy. Key points:

- Amazon requires sellers to match or beat its standard return policy (most product
  categories: 30-day return window).
- Health and personal care products (including OTC devices) may be subject to
  Amazon's no-return restriction for hygiene items. Apply for the hygiene exemption
  via Seller Central if applicable to your product.
- Amazon returns that include a safety complaint should still be logged in your
  complaint system (SOP-03), even though Amazon handles the return itself.
- Review Amazon's category-specific return policies annually; they change.

## 9. REFUND PROCESSING

1. Issue refunds to the original payment method only, unless the customer requests
   and you approve an alternative.
2. Process refunds within 5 business days of receiving and inspecting the returned
   product (or within 5 business days of approving a non-return refund for a
   defective product).
3. FTC requirement: if you advertise a satisfaction guarantee or money-back
   guarantee, you must honor it promptly. "Promptly" under FTC guidance typically
   means within 7 business days of cancellation/return.
4. Do not require customers to return the product before issuing a refund for items
   under $[25] where the cost of return shipping exceeds the product value (use
   your own threshold). Document the reason.
5. Log all refunds issued on Form LOG-REFUND-01 with the order number, refund
   amount, reason, and date.
