## 1. PURPOSE

To establish a consistent, documented process for receiving, logging, investigating,
and closing customer complaints related to OTC medical devices sold or distributed by
[COMPANY NAME]. This SOP also establishes the decision process for evaluating whether
a complaint requires an FDA Medical Device Report (MDR) under 21 CFR Part 803.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This SOP applies to:
- All customer-facing staff who may receive a complaint by phone, email, chat,
  or social media
- Operations and quality staff responsible for complaint investigation
- The regulatory coordinator responsible for MDR assessment

A "complaint" for purposes of this SOP means any written, electronic, or oral
communication that alleges a deficiency in the identity, quality, durability,
reliability, safety, effectiveness, or performance of a device after it has been
released for distribution.

---

## 3. COMPLAINT INTAKE

### 3.1 Receiving a Complaint

All staff must recognize the following as potential complaints requiring logging:

- Customer says a device caused pain, skin irritation, hearing changes, ear injury,
  infection, or any physical harm
- Customer says a device malfunctioned, stopped working, produced unexpected sounds,
  or failed to perform as described on the label
- Customer says the device was received damaged or appeared to have been previously
  opened
- Any report of a device-related visit to a doctor, urgent care, or emergency room
- Any report that a device was involved in a fall or accident
- Social media post, review, or direct message describing a product safety concern

Routine requests for returns, exchanges, or refunds where no injury or malfunction is
alleged are processed under the Return/Refund SOP (SOP-05) and do not require a
complaint log entry unless a safety or malfunction concern is identified during
processing.

### 3.2 Intake Script (Telephone)

Use this script or equivalent when a complaint is received by phone:

"Thank you for contacting us. I want to make sure we document this correctly so
we can follow up with you. May I get your name and the best way to reach you?
Can you describe what happened? When did this occur? Do you still have the product?
May I get the product name and the lot number or serial number from the packaging?
Have you or anyone else sought medical attention as a result of this?"

Record all responses verbatim in the complaint log (see Section 3.3). Do not
editorialize, minimize, or coach the customer. Your job at intake is to listen
and document accurately.

### 3.3 Complaint Log Entry

Open a new complaint log entry within 2 hours of receiving a complaint. Record:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Complaint ID | Sequential number (e.g., CMP-2024-001) |
| Date Received | Date complaint was received |
| Intake Method | Phone / Email / Chat / Social / In-Person |
| Customer Name | Full name |
| Customer Contact | Phone and/or email |
| Product Name | As labeled |
| Lot/Serial Number | From packaging if available |
| Purchase Date | Approximate if exact unknown |
| Purchase Location | Your store, Amazon, distributor, etc. |
| Complaint Description | Verbatim customer statement |
| Alleged Injury | Yes / No. If yes, describe. |
| Medical Attention | Yes / No / Unknown. If yes, describe. |
| Device Returned | Yes / No / Pending |
| Initial Staff Name | Person who took the complaint |
| Escalation Required | Yes / No (see Section 4) |

## 4. COMPLAINT TRIAGE AND ESCALATION

### 4.1 Immediate Escalation Triggers

Escalate to the regulatory coordinator or quality manager within 4 hours if the
complaint involves ANY of the following:

- [ ] An alleged physical injury (ear pain, skin wound, hearing change, burn, fall)
- [ ] Alleged medical treatment (doctor visit, ER, hospitalization) potentially
      related to device use
- [ ] A device that the customer describes as smoking, sparking, overheating, or
      catching fire
- [ ] A report that a device component was ingested (especially by a child)
- [ ] A death that may be related to device use
- [ ] A malfunction that, if it occurred again, could cause or contribute to a
      serious injury

For all escalated complaints, notify the regulatory coordinator and preserve the
complaint log entry. Do not destroy, alter, or return the product until the
regulatory coordinator has completed the MDR assessment in Section 5.

### 4.2 Standard Complaint Investigation

For complaints not meeting escalation criteria, the complaint must still be
investigated and closed within 30 calendar days. Investigation steps:

1. Attempt to obtain the physical device for inspection (offer prepaid return label).
2. Review the lot/serial number against production and quality records.
3. Determine if other complaints have been received for the same lot or SKU.
4. Document findings in the complaint log.
5. Determine root cause if possible (user error, manufacturing defect, shipping
   damage, labeling confusion).
6. Identify corrective action if appropriate.
7. Close the complaint with a documented outcome.

## 5. MDR REPORTABILITY ASSESSMENT

### 5.1 Who Must File MDRs

Under 21 CFR Part 803, device manufacturers and importers must submit MDRs to FDA
when they become aware of information that reasonably suggests that a device they
market may have caused or contributed to a serious injury or death, or that a device
malfunctioned and, if it were to recur, could cause or contribute to a serious injury
or death.

If your company is a device manufacturer or importer (you are registered with FDA
per SOP-02), this obligation applies to you.

If you are a retailer or distributor who did not manufacture or import the device,
you are NOT required to submit MDRs, but you are required to forward complaint
information to the manufacturer or importer within 5 business days of receipt.

### 5.2 MDR Decision Tree

Use this decision tree for every escalated complaint:

STEP 1: Did the event result in serious injury or death?
- Serious injury means: life-threatening condition, permanent impairment, damage
  to a body structure, requires medical or surgical intervention.
- If YES: proceed to STEP 3.
- If NO: proceed to STEP 2.

STEP 2: Did the device malfunction, and if that malfunction recurred, could it
cause or contribute to a serious injury or death?
- If YES: proceed to STEP 3.
- If NO: this event is NOT reportable as an MDR. Document the determination and
  close the complaint per standard process.

STEP 3: This event IS likely reportable. Notify the regulatory coordinator
immediately. File the MDR within:
- 30 calendar days of becoming aware of the event (standard deadline).
- 5 calendar days if the event requires immediate remedial action to prevent an
  unreasonable risk of substantial harm to public health (expedited report).

### 5.3 MDR Filing Steps

1. Log in to the FDA Electronic Submissions Gateway (ESG) at esubmitter.fda.gov
   or use eMDR (for electronic submission).
2. Complete FDA Form 3500A (mandatory reporter version).
3. Include: device identification, manufacturer information, event description,
   patient information (use "Patient A" or similar if name must be protected),
   outcome, and any remedial actions taken.
4. Submit and retain the confirmation number.
5. Retain a copy of the completed Form 3500A in the complaint file for 5 years
   (or for the expected life of the device, whichever is longer).

## 6. CUSTOMER RESPONSE STANDARDS

### 6.1 Acknowledgment (All Complaints)

Acknowledge every complaint within 1 business day:

"Thank you for contacting us about your experience with [PRODUCT NAME]. We take
all product feedback seriously. We have logged your concern and a member of our
team will follow up with you within [X] business days. Your reference number is
[COMPLAINT ID]."

Do not make admissions of defect or liability in the acknowledgment.

### 6.2 Closing a Complaint (Standard)

When a complaint is closed, send the customer a closing communication that:
- References the complaint ID
- Summarizes the finding without admitting liability
- States the resolution offered (refund, replacement, no action)
- Thanks the customer

Example: "We reviewed your experience with [PRODUCT]. Based on our investigation,
[BRIEF FINDING WITHOUT ADMITTING DEFECT]. We would like to offer you [RESOLUTION].
Thank you for bringing this to our attention."

### 6.3 Social Media Complaints

Do not ignore social media complaints. Respond publicly within 4 hours with:
"Thank you for reaching out. We would like to learn more about your experience.
Please contact us at [EMAIL/PHONE] so we can assist you."

Move the conversation to a private channel. Log the social media complaint in the
complaint log using the intake fields in Section 3.3.

## 7. TREND MONITORING

The regulatory coordinator must review complaint log data monthly to identify:

- Any single SKU with more than 3 complaints in a 90-day period
- Any cluster of similar complaints (same type of malfunction, same production lot)
- Any increase in complaints following a change in the product, packaging, or
  supplier

If a trend is identified, escalate to management within 5 business days with a
written summary. Consider whether a voluntary recall, field safety corrective action,
or labeling update is warranted. Document the review and outcome.
