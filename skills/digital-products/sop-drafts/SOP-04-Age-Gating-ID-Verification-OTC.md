## 1. PURPOSE

To establish consistent, documented procedures for verifying customer age and
identity before dispensing or selling OTC products subject to age restrictions,
purchase quantity limits, or identity logging requirements. This SOP covers federal
baseline requirements and flags where state law commonly adds additional obligations.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This SOP applies to all pharmacy and retail staff at [STORE NAME] who handle OTC
product sales at point-of-sale or behind-the-counter dispensing. It covers these
product categories:

- Pseudoephedrine (PSE), ephedrine, and phenylpropanolamine products (CMEA-regulated)
- Nicotine products (cigarettes, e-cigarettes, vaping products, and nicotine
  replacement OTC items in jurisdictions with age requirements)
- Emergency contraception (Plan B and generics)
- Certain dextromethorphan (DXM) products in states with age restrictions
- Naloxone (Narcan) and naloxone combinations in states with specific dispensing rules
- Any other OTC product designated by state pharmacy board regulations in your
  jurisdiction as requiring age verification

## 3. FEDERAL CMEA REQUIREMENTS (PSEUDOEPHEDRINE / EPHEDRINE)

The Combat Methamphetamine Epidemic Act of 2005 (21 USC 830) sets the federal
floor for pseudoephedrine sales. ALL retail sellers must comply regardless of state.

### 3.1 CMEA Quick Reference

| Requirement | Federal Rule |
|-------------|-------------|
| Minimum purchase age | 18 years old (some states: 21) |
| Behind-the-counter storage | Required. May not be on open shelves. |
| Daily purchase limit (per buyer) | 3.6 grams PSE base (regardless of package count) |
| 30-day purchase limit (per buyer) | 9 grams PSE base |
| ID required | Yes. Government-issued photo ID. |
| Logbook required | Yes. Electronic or paper. Buyer must sign. |
| Logbook retention | 2 years |
| Seller certification | Self-certification required for retailers |

### 3.2 Calculating Gram Limits

The gram limits apply to the PSE base ingredient, not the package weight.
Common package conversions:

| Product Example | PSE per dose | Doses/pkg | PSE base in package |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| 12-hour Sudafed (30 ct) | 120 mg | 30 | 3.6 g (hits daily limit) |
| 24-hour Sudafed (10 ct) | 240 mg | 10 | 2.4 g |
| Generic 24-hr PSE (15 ct) | 240 mg | 15 | 3.6 g |

Your POS system or electronic logbook should calculate limits automatically. If
using a paper logbook, staff must calculate manually using the PSE content on the
product label before completing the sale.

### 3.3 CMEA Sale Procedure

1. Customer requests a PSE product (products are stored behind the counter; customer
   cannot self-select).
2. Staff member requests government-issued photo ID. Acceptable: driver's license,
   state ID, US passport, military ID. Do not accept: student ID, membership cards,
   expired IDs (unless state law specifies otherwise).
3. Verify the ID is not expired. Verify the customer appears to match the photo.
4. Check the electronic logbook or paper log for prior purchases by this buyer
   within the current day and 30-day rolling window.
5. Calculate total PSE grams the buyer would have purchased if this sale is
   completed. If the total would exceed 3.6 g (daily) or 9 g (30-day), DECLINE
   the sale. Log the declined sale on Form LOG-CMEA-02.
6. If the sale is within limits, enter the transaction in the electronic logbook or
   paper log. Required log fields:
   - Date and time of sale
   - Product name
   - Quantity sold
   - PSE grams in this transaction
   - Buyer's name (from ID)
   - Buyer's address (from ID)
   - ID type and number
   - Buyer signature
7. Complete the sale.

## 4. OTHER AGE-RESTRICTED OTC PRODUCTS

### 4.1 Nicotine Products

Federal law (Family Smoking Prevention and Tobacco Control Act) requires age
verification for tobacco and nicotine product sales. FDA rule effective 2020 requires
verifying age for all nicotine products including:
- Traditional cigarettes and smokeless tobacco
- E-cigarettes and vaping products
- OTC nicotine patches, gum, lozenges, and inhalers

Minimum age: 21 (federal minimum under Tobacco 21 law, effective December 2019).
Some states have stricter requirements.

Age Verification Procedure:
1. For any nicotine product sale, request photo ID if customer appears under 30
   (the "check to 30" standard widely used to build in margin for error).
2. Verify ID and confirm age.
3. If buyer is under 21, decline and document the refusal on Form LOG-AGE-01.
4. No logbook or signature required for nicotine products (unlike CMEA), but
   POS age prompt documentation is recommended.

### 4.2 Emergency Contraception

Current FDA status: Plan B and generics are OTC for all ages with no age restriction
at the federal level. No ID check is required by federal law.

However: some state laws vary. Check your state pharmacy board rules. Store policy
may require placement behind the pharmacy counter for security purposes; this is
a loss prevention measure, not a legal age restriction.

Staff must NOT refuse to sell emergency contraception based on personal beliefs;
this may violate state pharmacy board rules and potentially employment law. Direct
any concerns to the pharmacy manager.

### 4.3 Dextromethorphan (DXM)

Some states (including California) have enacted age restrictions on DXM sales
(e.g., 18+). Federal law does not currently restrict DXM sales by age.

Check your state. If your state has a DXM age law:
- Apply the same ID verification procedure as Section 4.1.
- Post signage at the point of sale.
- Train all cashiers.

### 4.4 Naloxone (Narcan)

Most states have standing orders or pharmacy protocols allowing naloxone dispensing
without a prescription. Age requirements vary. In most states, naloxone is
available without age restriction given its harm-reduction purpose.

Check your state's standing order or pharmacy protocol for any age, ID, or
counseling requirements applicable at your location.

## 5. POS SYSTEM INTEGRATION

1. Your POS system should be configured to prompt for age verification for all
   products in restricted categories. Verify this configuration is active during
   setup and after any system update.
2. For CMEA products, the POS or electronic logbook system must integrate with the
   National Precursor Log Exchange (NPLEx) or a state-specific electronic tracking
   system if required in your state.
3. Test POS age prompts monthly. Document the test on Form LOG-POS-01.
4. If the POS system fails and reverts to manual mode, use paper logs for CMEA
   transactions until the system is restored. Do not skip the logbook requirement.

## 6. STAFF TRAINING

### 6.1 Required Training Topics

All staff authorized to sell restricted OTC products must be trained on:

1. Which products at this store require age verification or purchase limits
2. How to request and evaluate ID
3. CMEA daily and monthly limits and how to calculate PSE grams
4. How to use the logbook (electronic and paper backup)
5. How to decline a sale and what to say to the customer
6. Where to find the store manager when there is a dispute

Training must occur before a new employee handles restricted OTC products. Annual
refresher required. Document on Form LOG-TRAIN-01.

### 6.2 Refusal Script

Use this script or equivalent when declining a sale:

"I'm not able to complete this sale today. [For age: You need to be [AGE] to
purchase this product.] [For CMEA limit: This purchase would exceed the legal
limit for this product within the time window.] I'm required by law to decline
this transaction. I understand that is frustrating and I appreciate your
understanding."

Do not argue. Do not explain in detail which law applies. Do not be confrontational.
If the customer becomes aggressive, call the manager. Document all refusals on
Form LOG-REFUSAL-01 within 30 minutes of the refusal.
