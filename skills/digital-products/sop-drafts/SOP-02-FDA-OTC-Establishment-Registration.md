## 1. PURPOSE

To provide a step-by-step, plain-language walkthrough for completing FDA medical
device establishment registration and product listing via the FDA Unified Registration
and Listing System (FURLS) / Device Registration and Listing Module (DRLM). This SOP
targets small OTC device companies registering for the first time, adding new products
to an existing registration, or completing the annual renewal.

---

## 2. WHO MUST REGISTER

You must register with FDA and list your devices if your company:

- Manufactures, prepares, propagates, compounds, assembles, or processes a medical
  device in the United States for commercial distribution, OR
- Imports a medical device into the United States, OR
- Is the initial importer of a foreign-manufactured device

OTC hearing devices (hearing aids and PSAPs marketed as medical devices) fall under
21 CFR Part 807. Most OTC hearing aids are Class II devices (510(k) exempt or
requiring clearance) or Class I devices. Check the FDA product classification database
at accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfPCD/classification.cfm to confirm your
device's classification before beginning registration.

NOTE: If your PSAP is NOT marketed as a medical device (no disease or medical claims),
you may not be required to register. Consult regulatory counsel before making this
determination.

---

## 3. PRE-REGISTRATION CHECKLIST

Complete all items before logging into FURLS:

- [ ] Confirm your device's FDA product code (use the classification database)
- [ ] Confirm whether your device is Class I (most OTC hearing devices), Class II,
      or Class III
- [ ] Determine if a 510(k) premarket notification is required (Class II devices
      that are not 510(k)-exempt)
- [ ] Have your company EIN (Employer Identification Number) available
- [ ] Have your company's legal name, physical address, and mailing address ready
- [ ] Identify the Owner/Operator of record (the entity responsible for the
      establishment)
- [ ] Identify the U.S. Agent if the establishment is outside the United States
- [ ] Have the device product name(s) and intended use ready
- [ ] Have the name of any contract manufacturer or specification developer ready
- [ ] Pay the annual registration fee (check current fee at fda.gov/industry/
      registration-listing-user-fees; fee is assessed per establishment, not per
      product; fee waiver available for small businesses, see Section 4.3)
- [ ] Have a credit card or bank account available for fee payment via pay.gov

## 4. REGISTRATION WALKTHROUGH (FURLS / DRLM)

### 4.1 Create Your FURLS Account

1. Navigate to: https://www.access.fda.gov
2. Select "Create Account" and follow the FDA account creation prompts. You will
   need a valid email address. FDA uses Login.gov for identity verification; have
   a government-issued ID or financial verification method available.
3. Once your account is active, log in and select "Device Registration and Listing
   Module (DRLM)" from the My Portal page.
4. If your company already has a FURLS account (e.g., a prior employee registered),
   contact FDA CDRH at 800-638-2041 to be added as an authorized user rather than
   creating a duplicate account.

### 4.2 Complete Owner/Operator Registration

1. From the DRLM home page, select "Register a New Establishment."
2. Select establishment type. For most OTC device companies, this will be one or
   more of:
   - Manufacturer: if you manufacture (or contract-manufacture) the device in the US
   - Specification Developer: if you design the device and contract manufacturing
     to another party
   - Initial Importer: if you import devices from a foreign manufacturer
3. Enter your establishment's legal name exactly as it appears on your business
   registration documents.
4. Enter physical address (where manufacturing or specification development occurs).
   P.O. boxes are not accepted for physical address. A registered agent address
   in your state is acceptable if it is a physical location.
5. Enter mailing address if different from physical.
6. Enter the Owner/Operator official name (the individual legally responsible).
7. If the establishment is outside the US, enter the required US Agent information.
   The US Agent must be a person physically located in the US who is available
   during business hours.
8. Click "Save and Continue."

### 4.3 Annual User Fee and Small Business Exemption

1. Before completing registration, you must pay or qualify for waiver of the annual
   establishment user fee.
2. Current fee amounts are published at fda.gov each fiscal year (fiscal year
   starts October 1).
3. Small Business Fee Waiver: companies with gross receipts or sales of $1 million
   or less in their most recent tax year may qualify for a waiver. To apply, submit
   a small business certification via the FDA's CDER/CDRH fee portal. The waiver
   application must be submitted and approved before registration is complete.
4. Pay via pay.gov using a credit card, bank account, or ACH. Save the payment
   confirmation number. Enter it in FURLS when prompted.
5. Your registration is not complete until payment is confirmed or a waiver is
   approved.

### 4.4 List Your Devices (Device Listing)

After establishment registration is complete, you must list each device product:

1. From DRLM, select "List a New Device."
2. Enter the device name as it appears on the product label.
3. Enter the FDA product code for your device type. If unsure, use the Product
   Classification Database: https://www.accessdata.fda.gov/scripts/cdrh/cfdocs/cfPCD/
4. Select the device class (I, II, or III).
5. For Class II devices: enter your 510(k) clearance number (if cleared). If your
   device is 510(k)-exempt, select "510(k) Exempt" and cite the applicable exemption
   regulation.
6. Enter the premarket submission type: 510(k), PMA, De Novo, or Exempt.
7. Enter the proprietary name (trade name) and the common/usual name.
8. Select all operations performed at your establishment for this device.
9. Enter the contact information for the person responsible for this listing.
10. Click "Save and Submit."

Repeat for each distinct product. Each product variation that bears a different label
or has a different intended use is a separate listing.

### 4.5 Confirm Your Registration Number

Upon successful completion:
1. Your FDA Establishment Registration Number (FEI number) will be displayed and
   emailed to you. Save this number. It is required on your device labels if you
   are a manufacturer (21 CFR 801.1).
2. Your registration is valid through December 31 of the current year. Annual
   renewal is due between October 1 and December 31 each year.
3. Print or save your registration certificate from DRLM.

## 5. ANNUAL RENEWAL PROCEDURE

Registration must be renewed annually. Missing the December 31 deadline results in
a lapsed registration, which is a prohibited act under 21 USC 331. Do not let this
lapse.

### 5.1 Annual Renewal Calendar

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| September 1 | Set renewal reminder; verify current fee amount at fda.gov |
| October 1 | Renewal window opens. Log in to FURLS and begin renewal. |
| October 15 | Complete renewal. Do not wait until December. |
| October 31 | If small business waiver needed, submit certification by this date |
| December 31 | HARD DEADLINE. Registration lapses if not renewed. |
| January 1 | Lapsed registration is a prohibited act. Stop commercial distribution |
|            | until renewed. |

### 5.2 Renewal Steps

1. Log in to FURLS / DRLM.
2. Select your establishment and click "Renew Registration."
3. Review and update all establishment information (address, contact, US Agent if
   applicable).
4. Review and update device listings: add new products, deactivate discontinued
   products, update any 510(k) numbers or exemption citations.
5. Pay renewal fee or resubmit small business certification.
6. Confirm and submit. Save the renewal confirmation.

---

## 6. ADDING OR CHANGING PRODUCTS MID-YEAR

If you add a new device product or make a significant change to an existing listed
device between annual renewals:

1. Log in to DRLM within 30 days of beginning commercial distribution of the new
   device.
2. Select your establishment and click "Update Device Listing."
3. Add the new device following the steps in Section 4.4.
4. For changes to an existing device: if the change affects the intended use,
   product code, or premarket submission status, update the listing immediately.
   Minor labeling updates that do not affect classification do not require a listing
   update.

---

## 7. LABEL REQUIREMENTS TRIGGERED BY REGISTRATION

Once registered, ensure your product labels comply with these requirements:

- [ ] Manufacturer name and place of business on label (21 CFR 801.1)
- [ ] If you received a 510(k) clearance, do not claim "FDA approved" (clearance
      is not approval; use "FDA cleared" only if you have a 510(k))
- [ ] OTC hearing aids: include required labeling per 21 CFR 800.30
- [ ] PSAPs marketed as consumer electronics (not medical devices): do not use
      "FDA registered" as a marketing claim; registration does not imply endorsement
- [ ] If importing: importer name and address on label in addition to manufacturer

---

## 8. RECORD RETENTION

Retain the following records for a minimum of 5 years:

- [ ] Registration confirmation and FEI number
- [ ] Annual renewal confirmations
- [ ] Device listing confirmations for each product
- [ ] Payment receipts (or waiver approvals)
- [ ] Any FDA correspondence related to registration
- [ ] Screenshots of completed FURLS entries (recommended)

---

## 9. TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON ISSUES

| Issue | Resolution |
|-------|-----------|
| Cannot find product code | Search fda.gov classification database by device type. Call CDRH at 800-638-2041. |
| Duplicate establishment found | Contact FDA to merge or take ownership of prior registration. Do not create a new one. |
| Fee payment fails on pay.gov | Try a different browser. Use ACH bank transfer as fallback. |
| Login.gov identity verification fails | Use a government-issued photo ID verification pathway or contact Login.gov support. |
| Small business waiver denied | Review gross receipts threshold; provide prior-year tax return as documentation. |
| Registration shows as lapsed | Pay reinstatement fee and contact FDA to confirm reactivation before resuming distribution. |
