## 1. PURPOSE

To establish consistent, compliant, and customer-safe procedures for receiving,
stocking, merchandising, and reviewing over-the-counter (OTC) hearing devices at
[STORE NAME]. This SOP applies to personal sound amplification products (PSAPs), FDA
Class I and Class II OTC hearing aids (as defined under 21 CFR Part 800), and related
accessories.

---

## 2. SCOPE

This SOP applies to:
- Pharmacy technicians and retail staff handling OTC hearing device inventory
- Category managers responsible for planogram compliance
- Pharmacy managers conducting compliance audits

---

## 3. REGULATORY CONTEXT

The FDA's August 2022 OTC Hearing Aid Rule (21 CFR Parts 800, 801, and 808) created a
new class of OTC hearing aids for adults with mild to moderate perceived hearing loss.
These products are:

- Available without a prescription or fitting by a licensed professional
- Required to carry specific FDA labeling language
- Subject to FTC advertising rules (no claims beyond labeling)
- NOT classified as prescription hearing aids (those require audiologist involvement)
- Subject to state pharmacy and retail licensing requirements that vary by jurisdiction

PSAPs (personal sound amplification products) are a separate, older category. They are
NOT hearing aids under FDA rules and carry different labeling requirements. Staff must
be able to distinguish between the two categories to avoid miscommunication with
customers and misclassification on inventory systems.

---

## 4. RECEIVING AND INTAKE

### 4.1 Pre-Receiving Checklist

Upon receiving a shipment containing OTC hearing devices, the receiving staff member
must verify the following before signing the delivery manifest:

- [ ] Shipping cartons are intact with no visible damage
- [ ] Product UPCs match purchase order line items
- [ ] Lot numbers are legible on outer cartons
- [ ] Expiration dates (if present) are at least 12 months out from receipt date
- [ ] Temperature-sensitive handling instructions were followed (if applicable)
- [ ] Manufacturer return authorization numbers are noted for any refused units

### 4.2 Intake Documentation

1. Log all received units in the inventory management system within 4 hours of receipt.
2. Record: date received, PO number, vendor name, SKU, quantity, and lot number.
3. Flag any quantity discrepancies immediately to the category manager or pharmacy
   manager. Do not sign the carrier's receipt for a quantity you cannot verify.
4. Segregate damaged or suspect units in a labeled "Hold" area. Do not place them on
   the sales floor.

### 4.3 Labeling Compliance Check

For every new SKU entering the store for the first time, or upon receipt of a new
product revision, verify:

- [ ] Product label includes required FDA OTC hearing aid labeling language (if
      applicable): includes contraindications, self-fitting instructions, and
      the statement that the device is for adults with perceived mild to moderate
      hearing loss
- [ ] For PSAPs: label does NOT claim hearing correction, treatment, or improvement
      of hearing loss (this would constitute an illegal drug/device claim)
- [ ] Manufacturer name, address, and contact information present on label
- [ ] If a PSAP: label does not use the words "hearing aid"

If labeling appears non-compliant, place the product on hold and notify the pharmacy
manager before stocking. Document the concern on Form LOG-01 (see Section 9).

## 5. SHELF PLACEMENT AND MERCHANDISING

### 5.1 Location Standards

1. OTC hearing devices must be placed in a clearly designated section of the front-end
   or pharmacy department, separate from prescription hearing aids (if carried).
2. Shelf talkers and category signage must accurately describe the products as
   "OTC Hearing Devices" or "Personal Sound Amplifiers" as appropriate. Do not use
   the phrase "hearing aid" for PSAP products.
3. Price tags must match the current authorized retail price in the POS system.
4. No product may be placed on the sales floor until it has passed the receiving
   checklist in Section 4.

### 5.2 Planogram Compliance

1. Maintain planogram as provided by the distributor or category manager.
2. Conduct a planogram audit at least monthly. Record results on Form LOG-02.
3. Any out-of-stock position must be flagged for replenishment within 48 hours.
4. Facing counts, shelf heights, and product sequence must match the approved
   planogram. Unauthorized substitutions or re-sequencing must be approved in
   writing by the category manager before implementation.

### 5.3 Display Integrity

- [ ] Security packaging (if required by store policy) is intact
- [ ] Demo units (if used) are clearly labeled "Display Only" and not for sale
- [ ] Price stickers do not obscure required FDA labeling on outer packaging
- [ ] No handwritten claims or staff-added stickers claiming medical benefit

## 6. STAFF TRAINING AND CUSTOMER INTERACTION

### 6.1 Required Training (All Customer-Facing Staff)

All staff who may be approached by customers about OTC hearing devices must complete
training covering:

1. The difference between OTC hearing aids (FDA-regulated, for mild to moderate
   hearing loss in adults) and PSAPs (amplification, not medical devices)
2. What staff are and are not permitted to say (see Section 6.2)
3. How to direct a customer who describes symptoms beyond mild to moderate hearing
   loss to a licensed audiologist or physician
4. The store return policy for this category

Training must be documented. Log training completions on Form LOG-03 with date,
employee name, and trainer signature.

### 6.2 Permitted and Prohibited Staff Statements

PERMITTED:
- "This product amplifies sound. The label describes who it is intended for."
- "This is an OTC hearing device available without a prescription."
- "If you have questions about your hearing health, we recommend speaking with
  an audiologist."
- "Here is our return policy for this product."

PROHIBITED:
- "This will fix your hearing."
- "This is just as good as a prescription hearing aid."
- "The doctor says these work for [condition]."
- Any statement that goes beyond what is on the product label.

Staff must never diagnose a customer's hearing condition or recommend a specific
product for a medical condition. Direct all medical questions to the pharmacist on
duty or to an appropriate healthcare provider.

### 6.3 Pharmacist Consultation Escalation

If a customer asks about OTC hearing devices and also mentions:
- Sudden hearing loss
- Hearing loss in one ear only
- Ear pain, discharge, or recent illness affecting hearing
- Prior ear surgery

The staff member must offer to connect the customer with the pharmacist on duty,
who will recommend the customer consult a physician before purchasing any hearing
device. Document escalations on Form LOG-04.

---

## 7. INVENTORY MANAGEMENT AND LOSS PREVENTION

1. Conduct a cycle count of all OTC hearing device SKUs monthly.
2. Any variance greater than 2 units between system count and physical count must be
   investigated and documented within 5 business days.
3. High-value units (retail price over $75) should be reviewed for security tagging
   in accordance with store loss prevention policy.
4. Expired or recalled products must be removed from the sales floor immediately upon
   identification and quarantined per Section 4.2 hold procedures.
5. Manufacturer or distributor recalls must be processed within 24 hours of
   notification. Record recall receipt date, product description, quantity removed,
   and disposition on Form LOG-05.

---

## 8. QUARTERLY CATEGORY REVIEW

The pharmacy manager or category manager must conduct a quarterly review covering:

- [ ] Sales velocity by SKU (identify slow movers for reorder adjustment)
- [ ] Return rate by SKU (flag any SKU with returns above 10% for review)
- [ ] Complaint log review (see SOP-03 for complaint handling)
- [ ] Planogram currency (confirm planogram is still current from distributor)
- [ ] Staff training records (confirm all current staff are trained)
- [ ] Any labeling changes from manufacturers (re-run Section 4.3 check)
- [ ] State regulatory updates affecting OTC hearing device retail in your jurisdiction

Document quarterly review completion and findings on Form LOG-06. Retain records
for 3 years.
